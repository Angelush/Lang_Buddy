## Original

Twenty-second lesson

Time and time again...

— Can you repair my car by Thursday? — No, we’ll have to keep it till the weekend. It should be ready at ten o’clock on Monday morning. — Not until then? — Sorry, but we’re snowed under at the moment.
— From Tuesday to Monday: isn’t that a little bit long? — Well, there’s a lot to be done.
There’s the steering to check, the brakes to tighten... if you want it to pass the MoT, it’ll need all that.
In fact, perhaps it’s time to buy a new car. — Yes, perhaps, or time I found a new garage!

A modern music critic, talking to a classical music lover — Parson’s music will be played when Beethoven’s has been forgotten.
— Yes, and not until then.
— I’m sorry sir, the bar doesn’t open until five thirty. Would you like a drink while you’re waiting?

An English opera singer was invited to sing at the famous La Scala in Milan.

During the first act, he was very nervous but after his first aria the audience applauded and shouted for an encore. He sang it a second time and the shouts were louder. After the third time, he held up his hand and said:
— During my career, I’ve had many an appreciative audience but never anything like this. Thank you so much.

The conductor whispered — You don’t understand. They want you to continue until you sing it properly.
The tenor did not sing another note throughout the whole performance.

And since we are talking of music, a distinguished Irishman was invited to present the prizes to the children at his old school. In his honour, a choir of very young children gave an enthusiastic but off-key concert of Irish ballads.

Whenever they started a song, a lady sitting next to the visitor started crying.
— Are you Irish, too? he asked. — No, she replied, I’m a music teacher.
A lady with a broken nose said to a diplomat — You say you have never met an ugly woman — up until now.
The diplomat replied — Madam, you are like all women, an angel fallen from heaven. You just had the misfortune to fall on your face.

## Translation

Vigésima segunda lección

Una y mil veces...

— ¿Puede usted reparar mi coche para el jueves? — No, tendremos que dejarlo hasta el fin de semana. Debería estar listo a las diez en punto del lunes por la mañana. — ¿No antes? — Lo siento mucho, pero estamos desbordados de trabajo en este momento.
— De martes a lunes: ¿no es demasiado tiempo? — Bueno, hay mucho que hacer.
Hay que verificar la dirección, reajustar los frenos... Si usted quiere que pase el MoT, necesitará hacerle todo eso.
De hecho, quizás sea el momento de comprar un coche nuevo. — Sí, o quizás sea este el momento para buscar un nuevo taller.

Un crítico de música moderna, hablando con un aficionado a la música clásica: — La música de Parson se tocará cuando la de Beethoven haya sido olvidada.
— Sí, pero hasta entonces no.
— Lo siento mucho, señor, el bar no abre hasta las cinco y treinta. ¿Quiere una copa mientras espera?

Un cantante de ópera inglés fue invitado a cantar en la célebre Scala de Milán.

Durante el primer acto, estaba muy nervioso, pero después de su primer aria, el público aplaudió y gritó ‘¡Bis!’. Cantó por segunda vez y los gritos aumentaron. Al cabo de la tercera vez, levantó la mano y dijo:
— Durante mi carrera he tenido numerosos públicos muy entusiastas, pero jamás nada como esto. Muchísimas gracias.

El director de la orquesta susurró: — Usted no comprende. Ellos quieren que usted continúe hasta que lo cante bien.
El tenor no cantó otra nota de música hasta el final de toda la representación.

Y ya que hablamos de música, un irlandés distinguido fue invitado para presentar los premios a los niños en su antiguo colegio. En su honor, un coro de niños muy jóvenes ofreció un concierto entusiasta, aunque desafinado, de baladas irlandesas.

Cada vez que comenzaban una canción, una dama que estaba sentada al lado del visitante empezaba a llorar.
— ¿Usted es irlandesa también? — le preguntó. — No — replicó ella — soy profesora de música.
Una dama con la nariz rota le dijo a un diplomático: — Usted dice que jamás ha conocido a una mujer fea — hasta ahora.
El diplomático replicó: — Señora, usted es como todas las mujeres, un ángel caído del cielo. Desgraciadamente, usted tuvo la mala fortuna de caerse de bruces.

## Lesson notes

- **Not until then?**: Familiar for *Not before then?*. English uses *snowed under* (literally ‘covered with snow’) for being overwhelmed: *I’m snowed under with work*.
- **There’s a lot to be done**: = *There’s much to do*.
- **MoT**: Annual vehicle test in GB (*Ministry of Transport* test). Called *ITV* in Spain.
- **It’s time I...**: Structure uses past subjunctive: *It’s time I bought a new car* = it’s time for me to buy one. With *high*: *It’s high time he changed jobs* (more urgent).
- **Many a/an + singular noun**: Elegant form for *many*. *I’ve had many a great meal* = I’ve had many great meals.
- **Old school**: Former school. *Old boys/girls* = former pupils. *Old boys’ network* = alumni connections.
- **Encore**: French for ‘again’. *He played two encores* = he performed two extra pieces.
- **Off-key**: Out of tune. Opposite = *in key* or *in tune*. *What key is this song in?* = musical scale.
- **Up until now**: = *until now*. Can be shortened to *till now*.
- **Fallen from heaven**: Literary image. *Misfortune to fall on your face* = bad luck to land face-down (humorous).

## Exercises

**Sentence practice**
1. — Your appointment’s on the twenty-first. — Not until then?
2. Hurry up and finish breakfast. There’s a lot to be done before we leave.
3. Would you like a drink while you’re waiting?
4. The audience shouted for an encore.
5. You will continue practising until you are perfect.
6. The police are looking for a man with a broken arm.

**Fill in the missing words**
1. Ya es hora de que aprenda a conducir. → ______ ______ ______ ______ to drive.
2. Él está desbordado de trabajo hasta el mes próximo. → He’s ______ ______ with work ______ next month.
3. No hasta que Beethoven haya sido olvidado. → Not until ______ ______ ______ forgotten.
4. Yo he tenido extensas discusiones con Pedro. → I’ve had ______ ______ ______ with Peter.
5. ¿En qué tono usted toca esa canción? → What ______ do you ______ that song ______?

**Answers**: 1. It’s high time he learned, 2. snowed under ... until, 3. Beethoven’s ... been, 4. many a ... discussion, 5. key ... play ... in

## Vocabulary highlights

- **snowed under**: overwhelmed with work
- **steering**: mechanism for directing a vehicle
- **brakes**: device for slowing/stopping a vehicle
- **MoT**: annual vehicle inspection (GB)
- **classical music**: traditional Western art music
- **Parson’s/Beethoven’s**: possessive forms (music by...)
- **opera singer**: classical vocal performer
- **La Scala**: famous opera house in Milan
- **aria**: solo song in an opera
- **audience**: group of spectators
- **encore**: additional performance by demand
- **conductor**: person who leads an orchestra
- **tenor**: high male singing voice
- **choir**: group of singers
- **ballad**: narrative song/poem
- **off-key**: out of tune

## Patterns

| Form | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|
| not until | emphasizes delay | Not until then? |
| have a lot to be done | much needs doing | There’s a lot to be done |
| it’s (high) time + past | time to do something | It’s time I bought a car |
| make out | manage/cope | How are you making out? |
| pick up | recover/improve | Prices are picking up |
| sing off-key | sing out of tune | He sings off-key |

## Agent cues

- **Time expressions**: Practice *not until*, *it’s time*, *by Thursday*.
- **Phrasal verbs**: Focus on *make out*, *pick up*, *snow under*.
- **Music terminology**: Learn *aria*, *encore*, *off-key*, *choir*.
- **Cultural context**: Recognize *La Scala*, *Beethoven* as cultural references.

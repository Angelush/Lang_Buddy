## Lesson notes
- **(1)** Tras **can, may**, el infinitivo pierde la partícula **to**. No llevan «s» en la tercera persona.
- **(2)** Expresiones con **be** en lugar de **have**: 
  - I'm hot (tengo calor)
  - she's cold (tiene trío)
  - you're lucky (suerte)
  - to be right/wrong (razón)
  - he's hungry (hambre)
  - we're thirsty (sed)
  - I'm sleepy (sueño)
- **(3) From vs Of.** — From indica origen. (The door of my room vs I come from my room).
- **(4) At vs To.** — At indica situación; To indica movimiento. (He's at the post-office vs He goes to the post-office).
- **(5) Much vs Many.** — When a man has much money, he has many friends.

## Exercises
1 You are always wrong. — 2 The child is not sleepy. — 3 I'm afraid but I can't come. — 4 They are scared (or afraid) of the dark. — 5 Never mind. — 6 You know, he is right.

## Agent cues
Reinforce the 'be' + adjective states (hungry/thirsty). Practice the difference between 'from' (origin) and 'to' (destination).

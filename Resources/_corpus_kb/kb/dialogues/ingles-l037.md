## Original

Thirty-seventh (37th) Lesson

Directions

1 — Can you tell me the way to the British Museum? (1)
2 — Mmm... Let me see. Yes. Are you on foot?

**Prononciation:** 1... uel.. miusiám — 2... letmi sii...

3 — Yes, I am. — Well, go up Charing Cross Road and take Shaftesbury Avenue,

4 You come to New Oxford Street. Er, then it's, er, just opposite I think.

5 Yes, that's it, just opposite is Bloomsbury Street. — Sorry, say that again?

6 — Just opposite, you've got Bloomsbury Street. Go down there and it's on your right.

7 You can't miss it.

8 — Thanks very much. — That's okay.

9 — Have another beer. — I don't want one, but have one yourself.
10 — I will. I'm very thirsty. — You're always thirsty.
11 — Perhaps it's because I don't drink enough.

A glutton

12 (A mother to her son, after the sixth piece of cake):
13 — Tom, you are a glutton. How can you eat so much?
14 — I don't know. It's just good luck.

**Prononciation:** 3 ... gou áp chérin... roud... Shaaftsbéri... — 4 niu óksférd ópósi... — 7 .. Missit. — 10... ai uil — á glgtén. — 12 ...

## Translation

Lección treinta y siete

Direcciones

1 — ¿Puede usted indicarme (decirme) el camino para el Museo Británico?
2 — Mmm... Veamos. Sí. ¿Va usted andando? (¿a pie?).

3 — Sí (yo soy). — Entonces suba [por] Charing Cross y tome Shaftsbury Avenue.

4 Llega (viene) usted a New Oxford Street. Eh... luego, está, eh, justo enfrente, creo.

5 Sí, eso es justo enfrente está Bloomsbury Street. — Perdone, ¿me lo puede decir otra vez?

6 — Justo enfrente, tiene usted Bloomsbury Street. Baje por allí y está a su derecha.

7 No tiene pérdida (no puede usted dejar de encontrarlo).

8 — Muchas gracias. — De nada.

9 — Tome (tenga) otra cerveza. — No quiero, pero tómese usted una.

10 — Lo haré. Tengo mucha sed. — Usted siempre tiene sed.

11 — Quizá es porque no bebo suficiente.

Un glotón

12 (Una madre, a su hijo, tras el sexto trozo de pastel):

13 — Tom, eres un glotón. ¿Cómo puedes comer tanto?

14 — No sé. Es sólo (buena) suerte.

## Lesson notes

(1) To tell tiene varios sentidos, especialmente el de contar, relatar. To tell supone la idea de informar o dirigir a alguien. Aquí, lo traducimos por «indicar, explicar»...

## Exercises

1 Take the first street on the right. — 2 Turn left at the end of the road. — 3 It's only a twenty minutes' walk to Oxford Street. — 4 Sorry, say that again. I can't hear. — 5 Hurry up. Don't miss the train. — 6 Opposite the church you can see the school.

Fill in the missing words:

1 Hay tanto ruido que se va a acostar.
There is .. .... ..... that he .. ..... .. bed.

2 ¿Puede indicarme el camino para ir al museo?
Can you .... me ... ... .. the ?

3 Suba por esta calle y está a su izquierda.
... street and it's .. your ....

4 ¿Va (está) usted a pie o en coche?
Are you .. .... or .. a car?

5 Tengo suerte, no me pierdo nunca.
... le... get lost.

**Respuestas:** 1 so much noise - is going to. — 2 tell - the way to - museum. — 3 Go up this - on - left. — 4 on foot - in. — 5 I'm lucky - never.

## Agent cues

The directions dialogue (lines 1–8) is naturalistic with hesitations ("Er, then it's, er...") — excellent for prosody practice. Gap candidates: line 5 (learner repeats back "Sorry, say that again?"), line 7 ("You can't miss it"). The humour anecdotes (9–14) contrast with formal directions — use them for register shift practice. Note the authentic filler sounds in pronunciation; encourage learners to replicate hesitation markers.

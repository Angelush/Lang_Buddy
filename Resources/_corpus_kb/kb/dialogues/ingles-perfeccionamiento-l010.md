## Original

The anthropologist William Harding is to lead an expedition to Easter Island and the Maldives.

The Duke of Edinburgh and the Prince of Wales were presented to Queen Sophia of the Netherlands during a three-day official visit.

The salmon fishing in Scotland is famous — so famous that underwater fishing has been banned for fear of poachers.

Eliot Seidelman was a child prodigy. By the age of five he could play chess like a Grand Master and the violin like a god.

Acid rain is largely responsible for environmental pollution, as water samples taken from Lake Constance and the Lake of Geneva clearly show.

The England of Charles Dickens can still be found in certain areas of London.

The English and the French finally decided to build a tunnel under the Channel — two hundred years after the original idea.

American wine — and especially the wines of California — have recently made inroads into the European market.

Edgar Allan Poe's work is the best example of literary fascination with the supernatural.

Bill Hamilton, the Australian trade-union boss, was renowned as a tough negotiator.

He also had a glass eye that was so realistic that few people were able to tell it apart from the real one.

During a particularly difficult set of talks with the Secretary of State for Industry,

Hamilton agreed to give up a certain demand on one condition: that the Secretary should tell him which was the glass eye.
— The left one, replied the politician, without hesitation.
Hamilton was nonplussed. — Correct, he said, but how did you know?
— Easy, replied his opponent, it's so much kinder than the other.

## Lesson notes

**(1) Future infinitive in headlines**: *Lord Young is to head the enquiry* = L.Y. dirigira la investigación. Using infinitive for simple future is frequent in press headlines (see lesson 32). In normal conversation, use future tense: *The President will visit Moscow*. Also used for obligation: *You are to send me the report before Monday*. Negative more common: *You're not to open your present until Christmas Day*. Can use *must* in all cases.

**(2) Underwater fishing**: *Underwater* is an adjective, not a noun. Treat compound nouns as single units. *salmon fishing* (particular salmon fishing in Scotland) vs. *underwater fishing* (general). Compare: *I love pop music but the pop music they play on the radio station is too loud for me*.

**(3) To make inroads into/on**: Originally military (make incursions into enemy territory). Common meaning: to encroach. *The new Law and Order Act makes further inroads on individual freedom. Gambling made huge inroads into his capital.*

**(4) Tough**: Pronunciation: /tʌf/. *This meat's too tough to cut = Esta carne es muy dura. Tough luck! = ¡Qué mala suerte! He's a tough customer = No es fácil de manejar.*

**(5) Nonplussed**: = perplexed, surprised. More common in written language. Normally: *to be put out by*: *He was put out by the question.*

**(6) UK vs US government terms**: UK: *Minister* (Minister of Transport), exceptions: *Home Secretary* (Interior), *Foreign Secretary* (Exterior), *Chancellor* (Economy). US/Australia: *Secretary*. In US, *Secretary of State* = Foreign Minister. *A ministry* = un ministerio.

## Exercises

1. He is highly intelligent, as the results of the tests clearly show.
2. The cheese you are eating is one of the three cheeses produced in South Wales. — I've never eaten Welsh cheese.
3. I lost the bet. — Tough luck!
4. The Foreign Secretary is to meet his American counterpart the Secretary of State. The Home Secretary will also attend the meeting.
5. This lesson is so much easier than the other.
6. We generally buy white wines, especially the white wines of the Rhone valley.

Fill in the missing words:

1. The Scotland you describe is not___ Scotland that I know. *(the)*
2. They're identical twins. It's impossible to___ ___. *(tell them apart)*
3. No! He's___ ___ ___ than his brother. *(so much kinder)*
4. The___ English have much to learn from___ Scotland. *(-, -)*
5. Scottish people are very industrious.

## Vocabulary highlights

- **Easter Island**: Isla de Pascua
- **Maldives**: Maldivas (con *the*)
- **Duke of Edinburgh/Prince of Wales**: títulos nobiliarios (con *the*)
- **salmon fishing**: pesca del salmón
- **underwater**: adjetivo (pesca *submarina*)
- **acid rain**: lluvia ácida
- **to make inroads into**: hacer incursiones en, avanzar en
- **tough**: duro (carne), difícil, resistente
- **nonplussed**: perplejo
- **Secretary of State**: Ministro/Secretario de Estado (varía por país)

## Patterns

- definite-article-proper-nouns: *The* with island groups (*the Maldives*), none with continents/cities (*Scotland*)
- the-plus-adjective-nouns: *the* + adjective + noun for specific references (*the poor*, *the English*)
- future-infinitive-headlines: Infinitive for future in headlines (*is to lead*) vs. will in conversation
- underwater-compounds: Compound adjectives (*underwater fishing*, *salmon fishing*)

## Agent cues

- Notice how *the* is used with titles (*the Duke of Edinburgh*) but not with most proper nouns (*Queen Sophia*)
- Identify compound nouns and adjectives in context
- Recognize regional variations in government terminology
- Pay attention to idiomatic expressions (*tough luck*, *make inroads*)

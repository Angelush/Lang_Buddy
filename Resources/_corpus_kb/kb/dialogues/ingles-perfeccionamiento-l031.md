## Original

Thirty-first lesson

Careless talk...

1 A contributor to the satirical weekly
PUNCH once wrote about journa-
lists: (1) (2)

2 — You cannot hope to bribe or twist —
thank God — the British journalist; (3) (4)

3 but seeing what the man will do unbri-
bed, there is no real reason to.

4 Here are a couple of examples of what
can happen if you are not careful about
what you say to the press.

5 The British ambassador in Washington
was rung up by a local paper about a
fortnight before Christmas. (N 2) (5) (6)

6 — Mr Ambassador, what would you like for
Christmas? — I wouldn't dream of ac-
cepting anything. (7)

7 — Seriously sir, we would like to know —
and don't be a stuffed shirt. You've be-
come a media personality over the last
year. (8) (9)

8 — Well, if you insist, the ambassador re-
plied, I'd like a small box of chocolates
from Macy's. (10)

He thought no more about the incident
until Christmas Eve, when he was leafing
through a copy of the paper in ques-
tion.

A full-page feature article was devoted to a
series of interviews with foreign ambass-
dors in the United States. (12) (13)

— In this season of peace and goodwill, the
article ran, we asked three prominent
diplomats what they wanted for Christ-
mas.

The French ambassador replied — Peace
on earth, respect of human rights and
genuine freedom in the developing
world. (14)

His German counterpart called for a re-
vival in international trade in order to
generate worldwide prosperity and eco-
omic revival.

However, when we questioned the British
ambassador, he replied that he would
like a small box of chocolates from Ma-
cy's.

The Archbishop of Chicago arrived in
Britain for an episcopal visit.

In the VIP lounge at Heathrow Airport,
journalists from the different media were
waiting to question him. (15)

A reporter from one of the tabloids shou-
ted — Will you be visiting the strip-clubs
in Soho, Archbishop?

Innocently, the clergyman replied — Are
there any strip-clubs in Soho? He could
have bitten his tongue because the next
day's headline read:

Archbishop's first question: — Are there
any strip-clubs in Soho?

One can occasionally get one's revenge
on the press. When the Beatles returned
from their first American tour, (N3)

a journalist asked the singer John Len-
non — How did you find New York, John?
Lennon replied sardonically — I turned
left at Southampton.

## Lesson notes

(1) **PUNCH**: Revista satírica británica (fundada en 1841), derivada de *Punchinello* (marioneta italiana, conocida como *polichinela* en español). *As pleased as Punch* = muy feliz.

(2) *once* para acciones pasadas: *Oscar Wilde once said...*. Puede reemplazar *used to* para acciones habituales en el pasado (estilo literario): *I once thought I would be a painter*.

(3) **to bribe** = sobornar; **a bribe** = cohecho. En inglés americano: *kickback* (pago ilícito como reciprocidad).

(4) **to twist** = torcer (literalmente) o corromper (figurado, en este contexto). *Twist-off top* = tapa que se abre girando.

(5) **to ring sb up** = llamar por teléfono (sin preposición; *ring* = sonar). Alternativas: *to call*, *to phone*. *Give me a ring* = llámame. *fortnight* = quincena (14 días; no usado en inglés americano, donde se dice *two weeks*).

(6) *once* en este contexto no implica "una vez", sino un evento pasado sin relación con la frecuencia.

(7) **I wouldn't dream of + -ing** = no se me ocurriría jamás. Estructura formal; ej. *I wouldn't dream of lying to you*.

(8) **stuffed shirt** = persona excesivamente formal o pomposa (origen: *stuff* = tela; metonimia de apariencia vs. realidad). *Don't get shirty* (fam.) = no te enfades.

(9) **the media**: Colectivo singular/plural. En la práctica, el verbo puede ir en singular o plural (*The media is/are powerful*). *a media personality* = figura pública frecuente en los medios.

(10) **Macy's**: Famosos almacenes por departamentos de Nueva York (junto con *Bloomingdale's*).

(11) *as it ran* = como decía el artículo (lit. "como corría", refiriéndose al texto impreso).

(12) **feature article** = reportaje de fondo. *To feature* (v.) = destacar/presentar: *The film features Tom Hanks*.

(13) *a series* (singular; plural = *series*).

(14) **genuine** = auténtico/verdadero (no implica diferencia entre *real* y *genuine* en español; ej. *genuine leather* = cuero auténtico).

(15) **VIP** = Very Important Person (pronunciado /viː aɪ ˈpiː/ o /ˈvɪp/). *VVIP* = Very Very Important Person. En inglés americano: *veep* (col.) para vicepresidente.


## Exercises

1. He was jailed for three years for bribery.
2. I rang him up straight away to tell him the result; he was genuinely surprised.
3. The media tends to exaggerate the importance of small events.
4. One of the major features of the car is its reliability.
5. The poster read: Free Concert in Central Park.

Fill in the missing words:
1. Era tan feliz como un pachá. → He was ... as Punch
2. Una vez me dijiste que te harías escritor. → You ... told me you ... become a writer
3. Él no ha pensado jamás gastar dinero en regalos. → He has ... ... spending money on presents
4. La serie tiene dos actores célebres — ¡y una serpiente! → The ... ... two famous actors — and a snake!
5. Yo estaba hojeando algunos antiguos números de PUNCH la semana pasada. → I was ... ... some back issues of PUNCH last week


## Vocabulary highlights

- **PUNCH**: revista satírica británica; *as pleased as Punch* = muy feliz.
- **to bribe / a bribe**: sobornar / soborno.
- **to twist**: torcer (lit.) / corromper (fig.).
- **flak**: críticas (del alemán *Flugabwehrkanone*, cañones antiaéreos).
- **to gossip / gossip column**: cotillear / columna de chismes.
- **stuffed shirt**: persona pomposa/excesivamente formal.
- **media personality**: figura pública frecuente en medios.
- **Macy's**: famosos almacenes de Nueva York.
- **to leaf through**: hojear (un libro/revista).
- **feature article**: reportaje de fondo.
- **series** (singular; plural = *series*).
- **fortnight**: quincena (14 noches; no usado en EE.UU.).
- **to bite one's tongue**: callarse algo (lit. "morderse la lengua").
- **strip-club**: club de *strip-tease*.
- **genuine**: auténtico/verdadero.
- **VIP**: persona muy importante.

## Agent cues

- **Humor mechanism**: The lesson uses irony (ambassador's innocuous answer becoming a headline) and sarcasm (Lennon's *I turned left at Southampton* mocking the shallow question).
- **Media critique**: Satirizes how the press can twist words (*Archbishop's first question*) and the superficiality of some interviews (*How did you find New York?*).
- **Cultural references**: *PUNCH* magazine, *Macy's*, *The Beatles*, *John Lennon* are all iconic British/US cultural touchstones.

# CEFR can-do statements (quick reference)

## A1
### Speaking
- Can introduce yourself using basic phrases.
- Can ask and answer simple questions about personal details.
- Can use simple phrases to describe where you live and people you know.
- Can ask about people and things you know.
### Listening
- Can understand familiar words and very basic phrases about yourself and your family.
- Can follow simple, short, and clearly spoken directions.
- Can recognize simple words and phrases when spoken slowly and clearly.
### Reading
- Can understand familiar names, words, and very simple sentences.
- Can find specific information in simple everyday material, like advertisements or menus.
- Can understand short, simple messages on postcards.
### Writing
- Can fill in personal details in forms.
- Can write a short, simple postcard, for example sending holiday greetings.
- Can write simple sentences about yourself.

## A2
### Speaking
- Can describe aspects of your background and immediate environment.
- Can use a series of phrases to describe your family and other people.
- Can handle short social exchanges, though you may not understand enough to keep conversation going.
### Listening
- Can understand phrases and expressions related to areas of most immediate relevance.
- Can catch the main point in short, clear, simple messages and announcements.
- Can understand the main points of clear standard speech on familiar matters.
### Reading
- Can read very short, simple texts.
- Can find specific, predictable information in simple everyday material, such as leaflets and timetables.
- Can understand short, simple personal letters.
### Writing
- Can write short, simple notes and messages relating to immediate needs.
- Can write a very simple personal letter, for example, thanking someone for something.

## B1
### Speaking
- Can deal with most situations likely to arise while travelling in an area where the language is spoken.
- Can enter unprepared into conversation on topics that are familiar.
- Can describe experiences, events, dreams, hopes, and ambitions.
### Listening
- Can understand the main points of clear standard input on familiar matters regularly encountered.
- Can understand the main point of many radio or TV programmes on current affairs.
### Reading
- Can understand texts that consist mainly of high frequency everyday or job-related language.
- Can understand the description of events, feelings, and wishes in personal letters.
### Writing
- Can write simple connected text on topics which are familiar or of personal interest.
- Can write personal letters describing experiences and impressions.

## B2
### Speaking
- Can interact with a degree of fluency and spontaneity that makes regular interaction quite possible.
- Can explain a viewpoint on a topical issue giving the advantages and disadvantages of various options.
- Can present clear, detailed descriptions on a wide range of subjects.
### Listening
- Can understand extended speech and lectures and follow even complex lines of argument.
- Can understand most TV news and current affairs programmes.
- Can understand the majority of films in standard dialect.
### Reading
- Can understand articles and reports concerned with contemporary problems.
- Can understand contemporary literary prose.
### Writing
- Can write clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects related to your interests.
- Can write an essay or report, passing on information or giving reasons.

## C1
### Speaking
- Can express yourself fluently and spontaneously without much obvious searching for expressions.
- Can use language flexibly and effectively for social, academic and professional purposes.
- Can present clear, detailed descriptions of complex subjects.
### Listening
- Can understand enough to follow extended speech even when it is not clearly structured.
- Can understand television programmes and films without too much effort.
### Reading
- Can understand long and complex factual and literary texts, appreciating distinctions of style.
- Can understand specialised articles and longer technical instructions.
### Writing
- Can express yourself in clear, well-structured text, expressing points of view at some length.
- Can write about complex subjects in a letter, an essay or a report, underlining what you consider to be salient issues.

## C2
### Speaking
- Can express yourself spontaneously, very fluently and precisely, differentiating finer shades of meaning.
- Can present a clear, smoothly-flowing description or argument.
- Can take part effortlessly in any conversation or discussion.
### Listening
- Can understand with ease virtually any kind of spoken language, whether live or broadcast.
- Can understand fast native-speaker speech.
### Reading
- Can read with ease virtually all forms of the written language, including abstract, structurally or linguistically complex texts.
- Can understand complex manuals and specialised articles.
### Writing
- Can write clear, smoothly-flowing text in an appropriate style.
- Can write complex letters, reports, or articles which present a case with an effective logical structure.

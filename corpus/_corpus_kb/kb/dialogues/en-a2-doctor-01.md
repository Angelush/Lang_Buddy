## Dialogue

1 — Dr: Good morning, Ben. What is the problem?
2 — Ben: I feel terrible. I have a sore throat and a high temperature.
3 — Dr: When did it start?
4 — Ben: Yesterday evening, after I went to the gym.
5 — Dr: Did you take any medicine?
6 — Ben: No, but I drank a lot of hot tea.
7 — Dr: Let me check... Yes, your throat is very red.
8 — Ben: Is it serious? I have a big presentation tomorrow.
9 — Dr: It is just a cold, but you can't go to work. You need to rest.
10 — Ben: Okay. I'm going to call my boss on my phone now.
11 — Dr: Good. Drink more water than usual and stay warm.
12 — Ben: Thank you, doctor. I'm going to buy the medicine now.

## Pronunciation

- *Sore throat* — /sɔːr θroʊt/ (the **r** in *sore* links to the **th**).
- *Medicine* — /ˈmed.sn̩/ (the middle **i** is often silent).
- *Temperature* — /ˈtem.prə.tʃər/ (sounds like "tem-prah-cher").

## Translation

1 — Dr: Buenos días, Ben. ¿Cuál es el problema?
2 — Ben: Me siento fatal. Tengo dolor de garganta y fiebre alta.
3 — Dr: ¿Cuándo empezó?
4 — Ben: Ayer por la tarde, después de ir al gimnasio.
5 — Dr: ¿Tomaste alguna medicina?
6 — Ben: No, pero bebí mucho té caliente.
7 — Dr: Déjame revisar... Sí, tu garganta está muy roja.
8 — Ben: ¿Es grave? Tengo una presentación importante mañana.
9 — Dr: Solo es un resfriado, pero no puedes ir a trabajar. Necesitas descansar.
10 — Ben: Vale. Voy a llamar a mi jefe por el móvil ahora.
11 — Dr: Bien. Bebe más agua de lo habitual y mantente abrigado.
12 — Ben: Gracias, doctor. Voy a comprar la medicina ahora.

## Notes

- **(1)** **Have a [symptom]**: In English, we use *have* for most symptoms: *I have a headache*, *I have a cold*, *I have a temperature*. Note the article **a**.
- **(2)** **Past Simple**: Remember irregular verbs in the past: *go* → *went*, *drink* → *drank*, *take* → *took*. Questions use *Did*: *Did you take...?*
- **(3)** **Going to**: We use *be + going to + verb* for intentions or plans made at the moment of speaking: *I'm going to call my boss*.
- **(4)** **More than usual**: A common comparative structure. *Usual* acts as a reference point for the patient's normal behavior.

## Exercises

1. Complete with the correct past verb: "I .. (go) to the gym yesterday."
2. Fill in the symptom: "My throat hurts. I have a ____ ______."
3. Choose the right word: "I am .. to buy medicine." (go / going)
4. Reorder: water / more / than / usual / drink.

**Answer key:** 1 went. 2 sore throat. 3 going. 4 Drink more water than usual.

## Agent cues

Anticipation gaps: line 2 (*I have a sore ___*), line 3 (*When ___ it start?*), line 10 (*I'm going ___ call*). If the learner says "I have pain in the throat," correct to the pattern "I have a sore throat." If they say "I go to buy," model the "going to" future for planned actions. Maintain a professional but caring tone for the doctor and a tired, slightly raspy voice for Ben to reflect the sore throat. Use rising intonation for the doctor's questions in lines 1, 3, and 5.

## Dialogue

1 — Hi Alex! How was your weekend?
2 — It was great, thanks! I went to the new park in the city center.
3 — Oh, the one with the big lake? I saw your photos on Instagram.
4 — Yes! I walked there with my dog, and then we sat near the water.
5 — Did you take any good pictures?
6 — I did! I used my new phone. The camera is much better than my old one.
7 — Nice! Did you eat anything special?
8 — We found a small cafe nearby. I had a delicious sandwich and a cold juice.
9 — That sounds perfect. Was it expensive?
10 — No, it was quite cheap actually.
11 — Cool. I'm going to go there next Saturday.
12 — You should! It's the best place for a sunny day.

## Pronunciation

- *walked* — /wɔːkt/ (the **ed** sounds like a sharp "t").
- *saw* — /sɔː/ (long vowel, like "so" but more open; no "w" sound).
- *delicious* — /dɪˈlɪʃ.əs/ (the **ci** sounds like "sh").
- *expensive* — /ɪkˈspen.sɪv/ (stress on the middle syllable).
- *actually* — /ˈæk.tʃu.ə.li/ (sounds like "ak-chu-lee").

## Translation

1 — ¡Hola Alex! ¿Qué tal tu fin de semana?
2 — ¡Fue genial, gracias! Fui al parque nuevo en el centro de la ciudad.
3 — Ah, ¿el que tiene el lago grande? Vi tus fotos en Instagram.
4 — ¡Sí! Caminé hasta allí con mi perro y luego nos sentamos cerca del agua.
5 — ¿Sacaste algunas fotos buenas?
6 — ¡Sí! Usé mi teléfono nuevo. La cámara es mucho mejor que la de mi (teléfono) viejo.
7 — ¡Qué bien! ¿Comiste algo especial?
8 — Encontramos una pequeña cafetería cerca. Me tomé un sándwich delicioso y un zumo frío.
9 — Eso suena perfecto. ¿Fue caro?
10 — No, fue bastante barato, de hecho.
11 — Genial. Voy a ir allí el sábado que viene.
12 — ¡Deberías! Es el mejor lugar para un día soleado.

## Notes

- **(1) Irregular Past Verbs:** Some of the most common verbs change completely in the past: *be → was*, *go → went*, *see → saw*, *sit → sat*, *find → found*, *have → had*. You must memorize these!
- **(2) Regular Past "-ed":** For regular verbs like *walk* or *use*, we add **-ed**. Note that *walked* is pronounced with a /t/ sound at the end, not "wal-ked".
- **(3) Comparatives:** To compare two things, we use *adjective + -er + than* (e.g., *cheaper than*). For *good*, the form is irregular: **better than**.
- **(4) Actually (Stretch Item):** In English, *actually* means "in fact" or "really". It is a "false friend" because it does **not** mean "currently" (which is *currently* or *nowadays*).
- **(5) You should!:** This is a common way to give a recommendation. It means "te lo recomiendo" or "deberías hacerlo".

## Exercises

1. Change the verb to the past simple: "I (go) .. to the park and I (see) .. a lake."
2. Complete the comparison: "My new phone is (good) .. than my old phone."
3. Make a question: "______ it expensive?" — "No, it was cheap."
4. Translate the phrase: "Voy a ir el sábado que viene."

**Answer key:** 1 went / saw. 2 better. 3 Was. 4 I'm going to go next Saturday.

## Agent cues

Strong Anticipation gaps: line 2 (*I ___ to the new park*), line 5 (*Did you ___ any good pictures?*), line 10 (*It was quite ___ actually*). Correction strategy: if the learner says "wal-ked" with two syllables, model the one-syllable "walkt" sound. If they use "actually" to mean "now", explain the false friend. Register is casual and enthusiastic; use a rising intonation for "Nice!" and "Cool!". Look out for Spanish speakers saying "more good" instead of "better".

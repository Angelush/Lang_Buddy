## Dialogue

1 — Have you seen this? My news feed is blowing up about that "deepfake" news anchor scandal.
2 — Oh, the one on Channel 8? I heard they’d been using AI for weeks without telling anyone.
3 — Exactly. The network claimed that the technology was only used for "experimental purposes," but the staff were shocked.
4 — I bet. If I were a journalist there, I’d be worried about my job security.
5 — Apparently, several reporters have already resigned. They said they couldn’t work for a company that misleads its audience.
6 — Although the technology is impressive, transparency is crucial in media. People need to know what’s real.
7 — I agree. Despite their formal apology, many viewers are still boycotting the channel.
8 — If they had just been honest from the start, it wouldn't have turned into such a disaster.
9 — True. The CEO was interviewed this morning, and he insisted that no one was meant to be deceived.
10 — That sounds like damage control to me. He’s just trying to save face after being caught red-handed.
11 — Well, the story was broken by a whistleblower who leaked internal emails.
12 — It just goes to show that you can’t believe everything you scroll past these days.
13 — Definitely. I think I’ll stick to my newsletter subscriptions for a while; they seem more reliable.
14 — Good call. It’s getting harder to filter out the noise, isn’t it?

## Translation

1 — ¿Has visto esto? Mi muro de noticias está que arde con ese escándalo del presentador de noticias "deepfake".
2 — ¡Ah! ¿El del Canal 8? He oído que llevaban semanas usando IA sin decírselo a nadie.
3 — Exactamente. La cadena afirmó que la tecnología solo se usaba para "fines experimentales", pero el personal se quedó impactado.
4 — No me extraña. Si yo fuera periodista allí, estaría preocupado por mi seguridad laboral.
5 — Al parecer, varios reporteros ya han dimitido. Dijeron que no podían trabajar para una empresa que engaña a su audiencia.
6 — Aunque la tecnología es impresionante, la transparencia es crucial en los medios. La gente necesita saber qué es real.
7 — Estoy de acuerdo. A pesar de su disculpa formal, muchos espectadores siguen boicoteando el canal.
8 — Si hubieran sido honestos desde el principio, no se habría convertido en semejante desastre.
9 — Cierto. El director general fue entrevistado esta mañana e insistió en que no se pretendía engañar a nadie.
10 — Eso me suena a control de daños. Solo está intentando quedar bien después de que lo pillaran con las manos en la masa.
11 — Bueno, la noticia fue destapada por un informante que filtró correos internos.
12 — Eso demuestra que no puedes creerte todo lo que ves al deslizar el dedo por la pantalla hoy en día.
13 — Definitivamente. Creo que me quedaré con mis suscripciones de boletines por un tiempo; parecen más fiables.
14 — Buena idea. Cada vez es más difícil filtrar el ruido, ¿verdad?

## Notes

- **(1)** **Reported Speech:** Note the "back-shift" in line 2: *I heard they had been using* (Past Perfect Continuous) because the original action happened before the reporting.
- **(2)** **Conditionals:** Line 4 uses the **Second Conditional** (*If I were... I would be*) for a hypothetical present, while line 8 uses the **Third Conditional** (*If they had been... it wouldn't have turned*) for a hypothetical past regret.
- **(3)** **Discourse Markers:** *Although* is followed by a clause (subject + verb), whereas *Despite* is followed by a noun or a gerund (*Despite their apology*).
- **(4)** **Idioms:** *To save face* means to avoid humiliation or preserve one's reputation. *Caught red-handed* means discovered while committing a crime or doing something wrong.

## Exercises

1. Change to reported speech: Maya said, "The technology is experimental." → Maya said that...
2. Fill in the blank with *although* or *despite*: "__________ the high quality of the AI, viewers were angry."
3. Use the Third Conditional: "If the whistleblower (not leak) __________ the emails, the secret (remain) __________ hidden."
4. What does the phrasal verb "stick to" mean in line 13? (a) to glue something, (b) to continue with something without changing, (c) to attack someone.

**Answer key:** 1 ...the technology was experimental. 2 Despite. 3 hadn't leaked / would have remained. 4 (b).

## Agent cues

Lines 4 and 8 are critical for checking conditional accuracy; if the student says "If they would have been honest," correct to "If they had been." In line 1, ensure "blowing up" is understood as "trending/viral" not literal. Watch for the silent 't' in *whistleblower*. Encourage a skeptical but engaged tone, typical of friends discussing a scandal. Target L1-ES error: using "it results" instead of "it goes to show" or "it turns out."

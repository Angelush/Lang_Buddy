## Dialogue

1 — Marcus: Thanks for jumping on this call, Elena. I’ve had a look at the latest wireframes and, while they’re great, we’ve decided to pivot slightly on the user onboarding flow.
2 — Elena: I see. Could you elaborate on what "pivoting slightly" entails in terms of specific features?
3 — Marcus: Well, we’d like to integrate the social media API right at the start, which effectively means redesigning the login architecture. It shouldn’t take more than a couple of hours, right?
4 — Elena: I appreciate the need for agility, Marcus; however, what concerns me is that such a shift fundamentally alters the logic we initially agreed upon.
5 — Marcus: I was under the impression that the flat fee covered minor adjustments to the flow.
6 — Elena: Under normal circumstances, it would. Yet, given the complexity of API integration, it would appear we are moving beyond the scope of "minor adjustments."
7 — Marcus: So, what are you suggesting? I'm on a tight budget here, as you know.
8 — Elena: I’m sure you can appreciate that I need to ensure the project remains viable for me. Were I to absorb these extra hours, the quality of the final hand-off might be compromised.
9 — Marcus: I certainly don't want that. But I can't double the budget.
10 — Elena: Of course. Might I suggest a middle ground? I’m amenable to incorporating the API if we scale back on the custom animations for the dashboard.
11 — Marcus: That seems like a reasonable trade-off. It’s better to have a functional login than fancy transitions.
12 — Elena: Precisely. That being said, if you still require the animations later, we’d need to look at an addendum to the contract.
13 — Marcus: Fair enough. I'll have to run it by my co-founder, but I'm confident we can make that work.
14 — Elena: Excellent. Should you find yourself in agreement after your meeting, I’ll send over the revised project roadmap by EOD.
15 — Marcus: Great. I'll get back to you shortly. Thanks for being so flexible, Elena.
16 — Elena: My pleasure. I just want to ensure we’re both on the same page before I dive back in.

## Translation

1 — Marcus: Gracias por unirte a la llamada, Elena. He echado un vistazo a los últimos esquemas y, aunque están genial, hemos decidido cambiar un poco el rumbo del flujo de incorporación de usuarios.
2 — Elena: Entiendo. ¿Podrías detallar qué implica exactamente "cambiar un poco el rumbo" en términos de funciones específicas?
3 — Marcus: Bueno, nos gustaría integrar la API de redes sociales justo al principio, lo que en la práctica significa rediseñar la arquitectura de inicio de sesión. No debería llevar más de un par de horas, ¿verdad?
4 — Elena: Agradezco la necesidad de agilidad, Marcus; sin embargo, lo que me preocupa es que tal cambio altera fundamentalmente la lógica que acordamos inicialmente.
5 — Marcus: Tenía la impresión de que la tarifa plana cubría ajustes menores en el flujo.
6 — Elena: En circunstancias normales, así sería. No obstante, dada la complejidad de la integración de la API, parece que nos estamos alejando del alcance de "ajustes menores".
7 — Marcus: Entonces, ¿qué sugieres? Tengo un presupuesto ajustado, como sabes.
8 — Elena: Estoy segura de que entenderás que necesito asegurar que el proyecto siga siendo viable para mí. Si yo absorbiera estas horas extra, la calidad de la entrega final podría verse comprometida.
9 — Marcus: Desde luego no quiero eso. Pero no puedo duplicar el presupuesto.
10 — Elena: Por supuesto. ¿Podría sugerir un punto medio? Estoy dispuesta a incorporar la API si reducimos las animaciones personalizadas del panel de control.
11 — Marcus: Parece un intercambio razonable. Es mejor tener un inicio de sesión funcional que transiciones vistosas.
12 — Elena: Precisamente. Dicho esto, si más adelante sigues necesitando las animaciones, tendríamos que considerar un anexo al contrato.
13 — Marcus: Me parece justo. Tendré que consultarlo con mi socio, pero confío en que podamos hacerlo funcionar.
14 — Elena: Excelente. Si llegarais a un acuerdo tras vuestra reunión, enviaré la hoja de ruta del proyecto revisada antes de que acabe el día.
15 — Marcus: Genial. Te digo algo pronto. Gracias por ser tan flexible, Elena.
16 — Elena: Un placer. Solo quiero asegurar que estemos en la misma sintonía antes de ponerme manos a la obra otra vez.

## Notes

- **(1)** *Pivot* is a very common business term, especially in startups, meaning to change strategy or direction.
- **(2)** *Cleft sentences* (Line 4: "What concerns me is...") are used at C1 level to add emphasis or focus to a specific part of the sentence.
- **(3)** *Inversion in conditionals* (Line 8: "Were I to absorb..." and Line 14: "Should you find yourself...") is a hallmark of high-level formal English. It replaces "If I were to..." and "If you should find...".
- **(4)** *EOD* stands for "End of Day," a standard acronym in corporate and freelance environments.
- **(5)** *Scope creep* (implied in the dialogue) refers to when a project's requirements increase unexpectedly beyond the original agreement.

## Exercises

2. Transform the following into a formal inversion: "If you need any further assistance, please let me know."
3. Choose the correct word: Elena is [amenable / amoral] to changing the contract terms.
4. What does Marcus mean by "run it by" his co-founder? (a) Ignore him, (b) Consult with him, (c) Compete with him.

**Answer key:** 1. What concerns/worries me is the deadline. 2. Should you need any further assistance, please let me know. 3. amenable. 4. (b) Consult with him.

## Agent cues

Anticipation prompts: Line 4 (*What ____ me is...*), Line 8 (*Were I to ____...*), Line 14 (*Should you ____ yourself...*). Correction strategy: If the learner uses "if" instead of inversion, acknowledge it's correct but nudge them toward the formal alternative for C1 mastery. Register is strictly professional; ensure the learner uses a diplomatic tone. Common ES-L1 error: translating "on the same page" as "en la misma página" is correct but they might struggle with the nuance of "viable" vs "factible". Focus on the flow of the negotiation.

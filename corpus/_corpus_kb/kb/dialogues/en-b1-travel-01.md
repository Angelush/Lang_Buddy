## Dialogue

1 — Hey, Sofia! You’re back! How was your hiking trip?
2 — It was incredible, but I’m exhausted. I’ve never seen mountains like those before.
3 — I saw your photos on social media. That blue lake looked stunning!
4 — It really was. I spent the whole morning there, just enjoying the silence.
5 — Did you have any trouble with the trails or the weather?
6 — Actually, I got lost once because my GPS app stopped working in a deep valley.
7 — That sounds scary! How did you manage to find your way?
8 — I followed a group of hikers who were going to the same mountain refuge.
9 — Smart move. Is there anything you’d do differently if you went again?
10 — I’d definitely book the huts earlier. Some of them were completely full!
11 — If you go again next summer, I’ll come with you. I used to hike a lot.
12 — Deal! But you should start training now because the climbs are quite steep.

## Pronunciation

- *Exhausted* — /ɪɡˈzɔː.stɪd/ (the **h** is silent, stress on the second syllable).
- *Stunning* — /ˈstʌn.ɪŋ/ (short **u** as in *cup*).
- *Refuge* — /ˈref.juːdʒ/ (stress on the first syllable).
- *Steep* — /stiːp/ (long **ee** sound).
- *I’ve never* — /aɪv ˈnev.ər/ (ensure the **v** links smoothly to the next word).

## Translation

1 — ¡Hola, Sofía! ¡Has vuelto! ¿Qué tal tu viaje de senderismo?
2 — Fue increíble, pero estoy agotada. Nunca he visto montañas como esas antes.
3 — Vi tus fotos en las redes sociales. ¡Ese lago azul se veía impresionante!
4 — Realmente lo era. Pasé toda la mañana allí, simplemente disfrutando del silencio.
5 — ¿Tuviste algún problema con las rutas o el clima?
6 — De hecho, me perdí una vez porque mi aplicación de GPS dejó de funcionar en un valle profundo.
7 — ¡Eso suena aterrador! ¿Cómo lograste encontrar el camino?
8 — Seguí a un grupo de senderistas que iban al mismo refugio de montaña.
9 — Buena jugada. ¿Harías algo diferente si volvieras a ir?
10 — Definitivamente reservaría los refugios antes. ¡Algunos estaban completamente llenos!
11 — Si vuelves a ir el próximo verano, iré contigo. Solía hacer mucho senderismo.
12 — ¡Hecho! Pero deberías empezar a entrenar ya porque las subidas son bastante empinadas.

## Notes

- **(1)** *I’ve never seen* uses the Present Perfect because Sofia is talking about her life experience up to now. The specific trip details use the Past Simple (*I saw*, *I spent*).
- **(2)** *Stunning* is a common B1-level "strong adjective". Instead of saying "very beautiful," English speakers prefer these emphatic words to sound more natural.
- **(3)** *Used to* is used here for a past habit that no longer happens (Leo used to hike, but implying he doesn't as much now).
- **(4)** *Huts* and *Refuges*: In mountain contexts, these refer to the basic hostels or shelters where hikers sleep overnight.

## Exercises

1. Change to the negative: "I have seen mountains like those before." → "I __________ seen mountains like those before."
2. Complete the First Conditional: "If you _____ (go) again, I _____ (come) with you."
3. Fill in the vocab: "The mountain side was very ______ (vertical), so it was difficult to climb."
4. What did Sofia use to track her route?

**Answer key:** 1 have never / haven't. 2 go / will ('ll) come. 3 steep. 4 A GPS app.

## Agent cues

Line 2 is a prime Anticipation gap for the Present Perfect (*I’ve never ___*). Line 11 is perfect for testing the First Conditional structure (*If you __, I __*). Watch for the "Spanish L1" error of using the future in the 'if' clause (*If you will go*). Encourage the learner to use *stunning* instead of *very beautiful* to improve their register. The tone should be enthusiastic and friendly.

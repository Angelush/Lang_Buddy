## Dialogue

1 — Look, I've managed to bypass the organic stagnation on the 'Vesta' project by deploying an LLM to seed the app store with a few hundred "authentic" user reviews.
2 — Pardon? Liam, that isn't just cutting corners; it's a blatant violation of our professional standards.
3 — It's simply a matter of priming the marketplace. Not only does it nudge the algorithm, but it also provides the app with the social proof it deserves.
4 — It is the sheer lack of transparency that I find most unsettling. Should this come to light, our agency's credibility will be shot.
5 — Hardly had I finished the script when the downloads started spiking. Results speak for themselves, Elena; the client is over the moon.
6 — Be that as it may, we're essentially gaslighting potential customers. I'm afraid I can't just look the other way while you automate deception.
7 — Aren't you being slightly pedantic? In this day and age, "authentic" is a relative term in digital marketing. We're just giving them a head start.
8 — Hardly. What bothers me is that we’re compromising our long-term integrity for what is effectively a short-term vanity metric.
9 — Suppose the client finds out and is actually thrilled with the conversion rates? What then? Do we still take the moral high ground?
10 — That's beside the point. It's the precedent we're setting. Under no circumstances should we be fabricating engagement to inflate performance.
11 — I'll grant you it's a grey area, but calling it "fabrication" seems a bit of a stretch. We're just amplifying the projected user experience.
12 — It's not a stretch; it's the reality. I'm adamant that we either disclose this strategy to the board or pull those reviews immediately.
13 — So, it's an ultimatum, then? You’re really prepared to stall the whole launch over a few bot-generated comments?
14 — If that’s what it takes to uphold our values, then yes. Integrity isn't something we can just toggle on and off when it suits the quarterly report.

## Translation

1 — Mira, he conseguido superar el estancamiento orgánico en el proyecto "Vesta" desplegando un LLM para sembrar la tienda de aplicaciones con unos cientos de reseñas de usuarios "auténticas".
2 — ¿Perdón? Liam, eso no es solo tomar atajos; es una violación flagrante de nuestros estándares profesionales.
3 — Es simplemente una cuestión de preparar el mercado. No solo le da un empujoncito al algoritmo, sino que también le proporciona a la aplicación la prueba social que merece.
4 — Es la absoluta falta de transparencia lo que me resulta más inquietante. Si esto saliera a la luz, la credibilidad de nuestra agencia quedaría destruida.
5 — Apenas hube terminado el script cuando las descargas empezaron a dispararse. Los resultados hablan por sí solos, Elena; el cliente está encantado.
6 — Sea como fuere, esencialmente estamos manipulando a los clientes potenciales. Me temo que no puedo simplemente mirar hacia otro lado mientras automatizas el engaño.
7 — ¿No estás siendo un poco pedante? Hoy en día, "auténtico" es un término relativo en el marketing digital. Solo les estamos dando una ventaja inicial.
8 — Para nada. Lo que me molesta es que estamos comprometiendo nuestra integridad a largo plazo por lo que es, de hecho, una métrica de vanidad a corto plazo.
9 — ¿Y si el cliente se entera y resulta que está entusiasmado con las tasas de conversión? ¿Qué pasa entonces? ¿Seguimos dándonos aires de superioridad moral?
10 — Eso no viene al caso. Es el precedente que estamos sentando. Bajo ninguna circunstancia deberíamos estar fabricando interacción para inflar el rendimiento.
11 — Te concedo que es una zona gris, pero llamarlo "fabricación" parece un poco exagerado. Solo estamos amplificando la experiencia de usuario proyectada.
12 — No es exagerado; es la realidad. Me mantengo firme en que o comunicamos esta estrategia a la junta o retiramos esas reseñas inmediatamente.
13 — ¿O sea que es un ultimátum? ¿De verdad estás dispuesta a detener todo el lanzamiento por unos cuantos comentarios generados por bots?
14 — Si eso es lo que hace falta para mantener nuestros valores, entonces sí. La integridad no es algo que podamos activar y desactivar cuando convenga al informe trimestral.

## Notes

- **(1) Inversion for emphasis:** *Hardly had I finished...* and *Under no circumstances should we...* are formal structures where the auxiliary verb comes before the subject. This is a hallmark of C1/C2 proficiency.
- **(2) Cleft Sentence:** *It is the sheer lack of transparency that I find most unsettling.* This structure (It is + [subject] + that...) is used to focus on a specific part of the sentence.
- **(3) Vanity metric:** A business term referring to statistics that look good on paper (like likes or followers) but don't necessarily correlate with real success or revenue.
- **(4) Gaslighting:** While originally referring to psychological manipulation in relationships, in modern professional contexts, it is increasingly used to describe corporate deception or making someone doubt their perception of reality/ethics.
- **(5) Be that as it may:** A sophisticated connector used to acknowledge a point while immediately dismissing its relevance to the core argument.

## Exercises

1. **Rephrase using inversion:** "We should never compromise our values for profit." → "Under no circumstances..."
2. **Contextual meaning:** In line 12, Elena says she is "adamant". Does this mean she is: a) unsure, b) flexible, or c) refusing to be persuaded?
3. **Cleft transformation:** "The algorithm bothers me." → "It is the _________ that _________."
4. **Idiom check:** What does it mean if a client is "over the moon"?

**Answer key:** 1. Under no circumstances should we compromise our values for profit. 2. c) refusing to be persuaded. 3. It is the algorithm that bothers me. 4. They are extremely happy or pleased.

## Agent cues

Line 4 and 10 contain high-value inversions; ensure the learner places the auxiliary verb correctly before the subject. In line 6, monitor the pronunciation of "gaslighting" and "deception" to ensure the register remains professional and tense rather than emotional. The learner might struggle with the "Be that as it may" connector; prompt for its use as a pivot. If the learner uses "over the sun" instead of "over the moon," correct the idiom immediately. Spanish speakers often omit the "it" in cleft sentences ("Is the lack of transparency..."); remind them that English requires the dummy subject "It is...".

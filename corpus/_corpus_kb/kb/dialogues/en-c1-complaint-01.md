## Dialogue

1 — Good afternoon. I’m calling regarding my booking at the Nexus Hub this morning.
2 — Ah, yes. Mr. Sterling? How are you finding the workspace so far?
3 — Well, to be perfectly honest, it’s not quite what I was led to expect.
4 — I’m sorry to hear that. Is there a specific issue with the connectivity or the amenities?
5 — No, the Wi-Fi is perfectly functional. What I’m struggling with, however, is the fact that the 'Executive Suite' I reserved is currently occupied by a workshop.
6 — I see. It appears there may have been a slight synchronization error with the app’s calendar.
7 — Be that as it may, I specifically opted for the premium tier to ensure a quiet environment for a sensitive client call.
8 — I completely understand your frustration, and I apologize for the inconvenience. Would a standard booth suffice for the time being?
9 — Given the nature of the call, a standard booth is hardly an appropriate alternative.
10 — I appreciate your position. Let me see if I can rearrange the workshop’s location immediately.
11 — Seldom have I encountered such a mix-up at a venue of this caliber, Jordan. 
12 — You’re absolutely right to point it out. It was our oversight, and we must make it right.
13 — I’m glad we're on the same page. Might there be some form of compensation for the disruption to my schedule?
14 — Certainly. Not only will we waive today's fee, but we'll also credit your account for a future booking.
15 — That would certainly go some way toward smoothing things over.
16 — I’ll get that sorted immediately. Shall I walk you over to the quiet zone while we clear the suite?
17 — That would be appreciated. I trust this won't be a recurring issue.
18 — You have my word. We’re already looking into the software glitch to prevent any repeat.

## Translation

1 — Buenas tardes. Llamo en relación con mi reserva en el Nexus Hub de esta mañana.
2 — Ah, sí. ¿Sr. Sterling? ¿Qué le parece el espacio de trabajo hasta ahora?
3 — Bueno, para serle totalmente sincero, no es exactamente lo que me hicieron esperar.
4 — Siento mucho oír eso. ¿Hay algún problema específico con la conectividad o los servicios?
5 — No, el Wi-Fi es perfectamente funcional. Sin embargo, con lo que estoy teniendo problemas es con el hecho de que la "Suite Ejecutiva" que reservé está ocupada actualmente por un taller.
6 — Ya veo. Parece que puede haber habido un ligero error de sincronización con el calendario de la aplicación.
7 — Sea como fuere, opté específicamente por el nivel premium para asegurar un entorno tranquilo para una llamada delicada con un cliente.
8 — Entiendo perfectamente su frustración y le pido disculpas por las molestias. ¿Le bastaría con una cabina estándar por el momento?
9 — Dada la naturaleza de la llamada, una cabina estándar difícilmente es una alternativa apropiada.
10 — Comprendo su postura. Déjeme ver si puedo reorganizar la ubicación del taller inmediatamente.
11 — Rara vez me he encontrado con una confusión semejante en un lugar de este calibre, Jordan.
12 — Tiene toda la razón al señalarlo. Ha sido un descuido nuestro y debemos subsanarlo.
13 — Me alegra que estemos de acuerdo. ¿Podría haber algún tipo de compensación por el trastorno en mi agenda?
14 — Por supuesto. No solo le condonaremos la tarifa de hoy, sino que también abonaremos un crédito en su cuenta para una futura reserva.
15 — Eso sin duda ayudaría bastante a suavizar las cosas.
16 — Lo solucionaré de inmediato. ¿Le acompaño a la zona de silencio mientras despejamos la suite?
17 — Se lo agradecería. Confío en que esto no sea un problema recurrente.
18 — Tiene mi palabra. Ya estamos investigando el fallo del software para evitar que se repita.

## Notes

- **(1) Cleft sentences:** In line 5, "What I’m struggling with... is..." is a pseudo-cleft used to focus on the specific problem. It is more diplomatic than saying "You gave my room away."
- **(2) Inversion:** Line 11 uses negative inversion (*Seldom have I encountered...*). This is a formal, emphatic structure common in C1/C2 English to express surprise or criticism.
- **(3) Hedging and Modality:** Phrases like "It appears there may have been" (line 6) and "Might there be" (line 13) use modals to soften the delivery, maintaining a professional and diplomatic register.
- **(4) Idiom:** "Go some way toward smoothing things over" (line 15) means to significantly help resolve a tense or difficult situation.
- **(5) False Friend:** *Caliber* (calibre) refers to the quality or level of something, often used for institutions or people.

## Exercises

1. **Rewrite for emphasis (Cleft):** "The lack of communication bothered me." → "It was the _______ that bothered me."
2. **Apply Inversion:** Rewrite "I have never seen such a glitch" starting with "Never...".
3. **Nuance:** Use a modal to make this complaint more diplomatic: "I want a refund." → "I was wondering if I _______ be eligible for a refund."
4. **Vocabulary:** Which word means "to officially say that a fee does not have to be paid"? (a) waive, (b) credit, (c) disrupt.

**Answer key:** 1. lack of communication. 2. Never have I seen such a glitch. 3. might / could. 4. (a) waive.

## Patterns

- [[patterns/it-clefts]] — *It was [noun] that [verb]...* (e.g., It was the app that failed.)
- [[patterns/pseudo-clefts]] — *What I [verb] is [noun]...* (e.g., What I need is a quiet room.)
- [[patterns/negative-adverbial-inversion]] — *Seldom/Never/Hardly + auxiliary + subject + verb...*
- [[patterns/polite-requests-modal]] — *Might there be / Would it be possible...*

## Agent cues

Line 5 is the critical pivot where the complaint is articulated; if the learner sounds too aggressive, suggest the "What I'm struggling with" framing. For line 11, focus on the inversion—Spanish speakers often forget to swap the subject and auxiliary (*Seldom I have* vs *Seldom have I*). The register should be "firm but fair"; Jordan must be apologetic without being subservient. Watch for the 'suite' /swiːt/ pronunciation, as L1-ES learners often say /suɪt/.

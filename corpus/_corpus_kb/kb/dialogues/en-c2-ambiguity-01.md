## Dialogue

1 — It’s all voice-activated now. The house boasts unrivalled sensitivity.
2 — How fortunate. I’ve always found you a bit... low-resolution in that department.
3 — The sensors detect the slightest change in atmosphere.
4 — I’ve noticed. It’s grown quite chilly since I walked in.
5 — It’s the climate control that's optimizing for your arrival.
6 — Optimizing? I feel like I’m being processed, Julian, not hosted.
7 — The interface is designed to be completely transparent.
8 — Oh, it’s transparent, alright. I can see right through it.
9 — Don’t you find the minimalism... liberating?
10 — It’s certainly one way to describe a total lack of character.
11 — Everything is integrated. One single, seamless experience.
12 — A pity the same can’t be said for our conversation.
13 — You must be suggesting there’s a disconnect.
14 — Let’s just say I’m getting a "404: Emotion Not Found" error.
15 — Maybe you just need to update your expectations.
16 — I think I’ll just unsubscribe instead.

## Pronunciation

- *Unrivalled* — /ʌnˈraɪ.vəld/ (the stress is on the second syllable).
- *Seamless* — /ˈsiːm.ləs/ (the long /iː/ flows directly into the /m/).
- *Optimizing* — /ˈɒp.tɪ.maɪ.zɪŋ/ (gliding /aɪ/ in the third syllable).
- *Unsubscribe* — /ˌʌn.səbˈskraɪb/ (primary stress on the final syllable).
- *Interface* — /ˈɪn.tə.feɪs/ (be careful not to over-pronounce the second syllable).

## Translation

1 — Todo se activa por voz ahora. La casa presume de una sensibilidad sin igual.
2 — Qué suerte. Siempre te he considerado un poco... de baja resolución en ese aspecto.
3 — Los sensores detectan el más mínimo cambio en la atmósfera.
4 — Me he dado cuenta. El ambiente se ha vuelto bastante gélido desde que entré.
5 — Es el climatizador el que está optimizando para tu llegada.
6 — ¿Optimizando? Siento que me están procesando, Julian, no recibiendo.
7 — La interfaz está diseñada para ser completamente transparente.
8 — Oh, es transparente, de acuerdo. Puedo ver perfectamente a través de ella.
9 — ¿No te parece que el minimalismo es... liberador?
10 — Ciertamente es una forma de describir una falta total de personalidad.
11 — Todo está integrado. Una experiencia única y fluida.
12 — Una lástima que no se pueda decir lo mismo de nuestra conversación.
13 — Debes estar sugiriendo que hay una desconexión.
14 — Digamos que me sale un error "404: Emoción no encontrada".
15 — Quizás solo necesites actualizar tus expectativas.
16 — Creo que simplemente cancelaré mi suscripción.

## Notes

- **(1)** *Low-resolution*: Used here as a C2 metaphorical stretch to describe a lack of emotional depth or detail in a person’s character.
- **(2)** *Transparent*: A deliberate double-entendre. Julian refers to the "invisible" nature of the technology; Clara refers to the "obviousness" of his motives or the emptiness of the space.
- **(3)** *Chilly*: This refers both to the literal temperature (climate control) and the awkward, cold social atmosphere between the two characters.
- **(4)** *404 Error*: A common tech term used figuratively at this level to describe someone who is emotionally unavailable or "missing" a human element.

## Exercises

1. Why does Clara use the term "low-resolution" to describe Julian?
2. Explain the irony in Julian’s use of the word "integrated" in line 11.
3. In line 8, Clara says she can "see right through it." What are the two possible meanings here?
4. Rewrite line 15 using a different technological metaphor (e.g., related to "bandwidth" or "compatibility").

**Answer key:** 1 It implies he lacks emotional detail or depth. 2 It's ironic because their relationship is clearly falling apart and "disintegrated." 3 Literally seeing through the glass/interface, and figuratively seeing through a lie or a thin excuse. 4 Example: "Maybe we just don't have the bandwidth for this anymore."

## Agent cues

Clara's lines should be delivered with a dry, deadpan irony, emphasizing the double meanings. Julian should sound slightly pedantic and overly enamored with his gadgets. In line 4, model the sarcasm in *quite chilly*. In line 14, ensure the "404" is pronounced as "four-oh-four." Correct any L1 interference where the learner might try to use "transparent" only in the literal sense; push for the figurative "obvious" meaning.

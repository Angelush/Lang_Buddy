## Dialogue

1 — Oh, Sam. I see you've opted for the 'Post-Apocalyptic Chic' look for the living room.
2 — Very droll. It's actually a revolutionary minimalist aesthetic. The smoke adds texture.
3 — I'm particularly impressed by the way the smart-lights are blinking SOS in Morse code. A touch of genius, really.
4 — They're not blinking SOS; they're pulsing with the rhythm of my creative process.
5 — And the scorched curtains? Was that part of the 'vision', or just a happy accident?
6 — Naturally. I was going for a 'charred-edge' vintage vibe. It’s all the rage in Shoreditch right now.
7 — Of course. And the fact that your smart-fridge is currently blasting death metal at five in the morning?
8 — It's merely expressing its existential dread at being stocked with nothing but expired hummus.
9 — Truly, a triumph of hubris over hardware. You've really outdone yourself this time.
10 — Please, Alex. I can’t handle this much unbridled admiration before I've had my coffee.
11 — Seriously though, did you honestly attempt to rewire the main circuit breaker with a paperclip?
12 — 'Rewire' is such a pedestrian term. I prefer to think of it as 'guerrilla engineering'.
13 — Right. And the resulting blackout that’s plunged the entire street into darkness?
14 — A bold sociopolitical statement on our over-reliance on the grid. I’m basically an eco-warrior.
15 — Well, Mr. Warrior, your neighbors are currently forming a rather 'vibrant' lynch mob on the pavement.
16 — Splendid. I was hoping for an audience to witness my next breakthrough.
17 — You’re a marvel, Sam. A walking, talking insurance liability.
18 — I aim to please. Now, do you happen to have a torch, or are we going to commune with the void in silence?

## Translation

1 — Oh, Sam. Veo que has optado por el estilo "Chic Postapocalíptico" para el salón.
2 — Qué gracioso. En realidad es una estética minimalista revolucionaria. El humo añade textura.
3 — Me impresiona especialmente la forma en que las luces inteligentes parpadean SOS en código Morse. Un toque de genialidad, de verdad.
4 — No están parpadeando SOS; están pulsando al ritmo de mi proceso creativo.
5 — ¿Y las cortinas chamuscadas? ¿Eso era parte de la "visión" o solo un accidente afortunado?
6 — Naturalmente. Buscaba un aire vintage de "bordes carbonizados". Está muy de moda en Shoreditch ahora mismo.
7 — Por supuesto. ¿Y el hecho de que tu nevera inteligente esté a todo volumen con death metal a las cinco de la mañana?
8 — Simplemente está expresando su angustia existencial por estar llena de nada más que hummus caducado.
9 — Verdaderamente, un triunfo de la arrogancia sobre el hardware. Te has superado a ti mismo esta vez.
10 — Por favor, Alex. No puedo manejar tanta admiración desenfrenada antes de haberme tomado el café.
11 — Pero en serio, ¿de verdad intentaste recablear el interruptor principal con un clip?
12 — "Recablear" es un término tan vulgar. Prefiero pensar en ello como "ingeniería de guerrilla".
13 — Ya. ¿Y el apagón resultante que ha sumido a toda la calle en la oscuridad?
14 — Una audaz declaración sociopolítica sobre nuestra excesiva dependencia de la red eléctrica. Soy básicamente un eco-guerrero.
15 — Bueno, Sr. Guerrero, tus vecinos están formando ahora mismo una turba bastante "vibrante" en la acera.
16 — Espléndido. Esperaba tener público para presenciar mi próximo gran avance.
17 — Eres una maravilla, Sam. Una responsabilidad civil andante.
18 — Mi intención es complacer. Ahora, ¿por casualidad tienes una linterna, o vamos a entrar en comunión con el vacío en silencio?

## Notes

- **(1)** *Droll* (line 2) is a sophisticated C2 adjective. It means "amusing in a
  wry or odd way." When used sarcastically, as Sam does here, it acknowledges
  the joke while dismissing it.
- **(2)** *All the rage* (line 6) is an idiom for being extremely fashionable or
  popular. Sam uses it to justify his disaster as a trendy choice.
- **(3)** *Pedestrian* (line 12) in this context doesn't mean "someone walking."
  At a C2 level, it describes something lacklustre, ordinary, or uninspired.
- **(4)** *Insurance liability* (line 17) is a common way to describe someone
  whose behavior is so risky that they are a financial or safety danger to
  others.
- **(5)** *Commune with the void* (line 18) is a highly figurative, almost
  poetic way of saying "sitting in the dark/nothingness."

## Exercises

1. Which word does Sam use to mock Alex's "ordinary" vocabulary choice?
2. Rewrite line 9 using "hubris" in a way that suggests a negative outcome.
3. Identify the hyperbolic phrase Alex uses to describe Sam's dangerous behavior.
4. Explain why "happy accident" in line 5 is ironic in this context.

**Answer key:** 1 Pedestrian. 2 His hubris led to the total destruction of the system. 3 "A walking, talking insurance liability." 4 It’s ironic because the "accident" (burning curtains) is clearly a disaster, not something "happy" or fortunate.

The learner must maintain a deadpan, ironic tone—avoid over-acting the humor; the dryer, the better. Correct ES-L1 errors like using "funny" instead of "droll" or "ordinary" instead of "pedestrian."

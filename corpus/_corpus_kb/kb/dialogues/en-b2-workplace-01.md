## Dialogue

1 — Marcus: Elena, I’ve been informed that the dev team wants to push back the release date by two weeks.
2 — Elena: That's correct. Although I understand the pressure, we can't launch with that synchronization bug.
3 — Marcus: I see your point, but if we were to delay it now, we would lose our primary marketing window.
4 — Elena: Nevertheless, the app would be heavily criticized if users' data wasn't saved properly across devices.
5 — Marcus: Couldn't the patch be released a few days later? The deadline was set in stone months ago.
6 — Elena: I wish I could agree, but our reputation is at stake. Had we known about this earlier, we might have pivoted.
7 — Marcus: Despite the risks, the board expects the launch on Friday. They've already spent thousands on ads.
8 — Elena: I've already spoken to the CTO, and she mentioned that the stability of the platform is non-negotiable.
9 — Marcus: So, are you suggesting that we call off the press event scheduled for Thursday?
10 — Elena: Precisely. It would be better to reschedule than to face a wave of negative reviews on day one.
11 — Marcus: Well, I suppose I'll have to break the news to the marketing department then.
12 — Elena: Thank you, Marcus. I'll make sure the devs work around the clock to get it sorted by the new date.
13 — Marcus: I'll back you up in the meeting, but let's hope the investors don't pull the plug on the funding.
14 — Elena: I appreciate that. Let's touch base tomorrow morning to finalize the new timeline.

## Translation

1 — Marcus: Elena, me han informado de que el equipo de desarrollo quiere posponer la fecha de lanzamiento dos semanas.
2 — Elena: Es correcto. Aunque entiendo la presión, no podemos lanzar con ese error de sincronización.
3 — Marcus: Entiendo tu punto, pero si lo retrasáramos ahora, perderíamos nuestra ventana de marketing principal.
4 — Elena: No obstante, la aplicación sería duramente criticada si los datos de los usuarios no se guardaran correctamente en todos los dispositivos.
5 — Marcus: ¿No podría lanzarse el parche unos días después? La fecha límite se fijó en firme hace meses.
6 — Elena: Ojalá pudiera estar de acuerdo, pero nuestra reputación está en juego. Si hubiéramos sabido esto antes, podríamos haber pivotado.
7 — Marcus: A pesar de los riesgos, la junta espera el lanzamiento el viernes. Ya han gastado miles en anuncios.
8 — Elena: Ya he hablado con la CTO y ella mencionó que la estabilidad de la plataforma es innegociable.
9 — Marcus: Entonces, ¿estás sugiriendo que cancelemos el evento de prensa programado para el jueves?
10 — Elena: Precisamente. Sería mejor reprogramar que enfrentarse a una oleada de críticas negativas el primer día.
11 — Marcus: Bueno, supongo que tendré que darle la noticia al departamento de marketing, entonces.
12 — Elena: Gracias, Marcus. Me aseguraré de que los desarrolladores trabajen día y noche para solucionarlo para la nueva fecha.
13 — Marcus: Te apoyaré en la reunión, pero esperemos que los inversores no retiren la financiación.
14 — Elena: Te lo agradezco. Estemos en contacto mañana por la mañana para finalizar el nuevo cronograma.

## Notes

- **(1)** *Push back* is a common phrasal verb in business meaning to delay or postpone a deadline.
- **(2)** *Had we known... we might have* (Line 6) is an inverted third conditional. It is a more formal way of saying "If we had known". This is a high-level (C1) structure used here to add emphasis.
- **(3)** *Non-negotiable* describes something that cannot be changed or discussed. In Spanish, it translates directly to *innegociable*.
- **(4)** *Pull the plug* is an idiom meaning to stop an activity or withdraw support/funding, like pulling the power cord of a machine to stop it.
- **(5)** *Touch base* is a very common workplace expression meaning to make contact or talk briefly to stay updated.

## Exercises

1. Complete the second conditional: "If we (delay) ______ the launch, we (lose) ______ our window."
2. Rewrite using inversion: "If they had invested more, the project would have succeeded." → "______ they ______ more, the project would have succeeded."
3. Choose the correct phrasal verb: "We need to (call off / break the news) the meeting because the CEO is sick."
4. What does "at stake" mean in Line 6? a) Guaranteed, b) In danger, c) Finished.

**Answer key:** 1 delayed / would lose. 2 Had they invested. 3 call off. 4 b) In danger.

## Agent cues

Anticipation prompts: Line 3 (*If we __ to delay it...*), Line 6 (*Had we ___ about this...*), Line 13 (*pull the ___ on the funding*). Correction strategy: If the student uses "postpone" instead of "push back", acknowledge it's correct but encourage the phrasal verb for a more natural workplace register. Prosody: Marcus should sound slightly stressed but professional; Elena should sound firm but empathetic. Common ES-L1 error: Watch for the use of "the Friday" instead of just "on Friday" in line 7.

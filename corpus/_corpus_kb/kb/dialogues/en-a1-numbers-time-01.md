## Dialogue

1 — Hi Mia! What time is it?
2 — It's nine forty-five. Why?
3 — Oh no! My Zoom meeting is at ten o'clock.
4 — You have fifteen minutes. It's okay.
5 — Good. Is that your new smartphone?
6 — Yes, it is. It's very fast.
7 — What is your phone number for the app?
8 — It's zero-seven-two, five-five-one-eight.
9 — Zero-seven-two, five-five-one-eight... perfect!
10 — See you later, Leo.

## Pronunciation

- *Time* — /taɪm/ (ensure the long **i** sound).
- *Forty-five* — /ˌfɔːrti ˈfaɪv/.
- *O'clock* — /əˈklɒk/ (the **o'** is a very short "uh" sound).
- *Zero* — /ˈzɪərəʊ/ (in phone numbers, some speakers say "O" /oʊ/).
- *Fifteen* — /ˌfɪfˈtiːn/ (stress the second syllable to distinguish from *fifty*).

## Translation

1 — ¡Hola Mia! ¿Qué hora es?
2 — Son las nueve y cuarenta y cinco. ¿Por qué?
3 — ¡Oh no! Mi reunión de Zoom es a las diez en punto.
4 — Tienes quince minutos. Está bien.
5 — Bien. ¿Es ese tu nuevo smartphone?
6 — Sí, lo es. Es muy rápido.
7 — ¿Cuál es tu número de teléfono para la app?
8 — Es el cero-siete-dos, cinco-cinco-uno-ocho.
9 — Cero-siete-dos, cinco-cinco-uno-ocho... ¡perfecto!
10 — Hasta luego, Leo.

## Notes

- **(1)** *What time is it?* is the standard question for the time. Unlike Spanish (*¿Qué hora es?*), we always use the singular *is* and the pronoun *it*.
- **(2)** *O'clock* is used only for exact hours (10:00, 2:00). You cannot say "ten thirty o'clock".
- **(3)** When giving phone numbers, English speakers usually say each digit individually. For 0, you can say *zero* or *O* (like the letter).
- **(4)** To say at what time something happens, use the preposition **at**: *at ten o'clock*.

## Exercises

1. Write the time in English for "9:00".
2. Complete the sentence: My class is .. nine forty-five.
3. Make it a question: "It is ten o'clock." → "____ ____ it ten o'clock?"
4. Listen and write the digits: zero-four-one, nine-three-six-two.

**Answer key:** 1 Nine o'clock. 2 at. 3 Is it. 4 041 9362.

## Agent cues

Line 1 is a prime candidate for an anticipation gap (*What ___ is it?*). For line 8, ensure the learner reads the phone number digits clearly and at a steady pace. If the learner uses the Spanish structure "the nine forty-five", gently correct by modeling "It's nine forty-five" (no article). Keep the register friendly and the pace slow for A1 comprehension.

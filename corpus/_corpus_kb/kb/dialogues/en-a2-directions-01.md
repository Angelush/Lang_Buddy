## Dialogue

1 — Excuse me, is there a museum near here?
2 — Yes, the City Museum. Go straight and turn right at the traffic lights.
3 — At the traffic lights?
4 — Yes. Then turn left at the bank.
5 — Is it far from here?
6 — No, it's only two minutes on foot. There's a café next to it.
7 — Can I walk there?
8 — Yes, you can. It's very easy.
9 — Thank you!
10 — You're welcome.

## Pronunciation

- *City Museum* — /ˈsɪti ˈmjuːziəm/ (stress on first syllable of each word).
- *traffic lights* — /ˈtræfɪk laɪts/ (the **c** sounds like /k/).
- *straight* — /streɪt/ (long **a** sound, like *ate*).
- *bank* — /bæŋk/ (financial institution; same spelling as river bank but different meaning).
- *on foot* — /ɒn fʊt/ (not *by foot*).
- *There's* — /ðɛərz/ (contraction: *there is*).

## Translation

1 — Disculpe, ¿hay un museo cerca de aquí?
2 — Sí, el Museo de la Ciudad. Vaya recto y gire a la derecha en el semáforo.
3 — ¿En el semáforo?
4 — Sí. Luego gire a la izquierda en el banco.
5 — ¿Está lejos de aquí?
6 — No, solo están a dos minutos a pie. Hay un café al lado.
7 — ¿Puedo ir caminando?
8 — Sí, puede. Es muy fácil.
9 — ¡Gracias!
10 — De nada.

## Notes

- **(2)** Imperatives for directions: *Go straight, turn left/right*. English omits the subject for commands. Spanish uses the imperative too (*Vaya recto, gire a la izquierda*).
- **(4)** *At the traffic lights*: use **at** for points (lights, bank, stop sign). Spanish *en* can mislead learners to use *in* incorrectly.
- **(6)** *There's a café*: **There is** for singular existence. Negative: *There isn't*; question: *Is there...?*. Spanish *hay* covers all, but English requires different forms.
- **(7)** *Can I walk there?*: Modal **can** for ability/permission. Spanish *poder* ("¿Puedo...?") is a true friend here.
- **(8)** *You can*: Affirmative short answer with **can**. No **do** auxiliary: not *"Yes, you do can"*.

## Exercises

1. Complete: "Go ______ and turn left at the bank."
2. Make negative: "It is far." → "It ______ far."
3. Ask about existence: "______ there a museum near here?"
4. Reorder: at / turn / the / bank / left → ?
5. Answer: Where is the museum? → "It's ______ here."

**Answer key:** 1 straight. 2 isn't. 3 Is. 4 Turn left at the bank. 5 near

## Dialogue

1 — Hello. I've just gone up to my room, but there’s a problem.
2 — I’m sorry to hear that, sir. What’s the matter?
3 — I've noticed that the air conditioning doesn't work, and the room is quite noisy.
4 — Did you try adjusting the remote on the wall?
5 — Yes, I tried that, but nothing happened.
6 — Also, I booked a room which has a view of the park, but this one looks onto the bins.
7 — Let me check your booking on our app... Ah, I see the issue.
8 — There has been a mistake in our system; we should have given you room 402.
9 — Is it possible to move now? I have to work tomorrow and I need a quiet space.
10 — If you wait a moment, I’ll check if the cleaning staff have finished that room.
11 — Yes, it’s ready. Here is your new key card for 402.
12 — Great. Does this room have the high-speed Wi-Fi that I saw on your website?
13 — Yes, it does. And we’ve upgraded your breakfast for the trouble.
14 — Thank you, that’s very kind of you.

## Pronunciation

- *Noisy* — /ˈnɔɪ.zi/ (voiced **z** sound, not "noissy").
- *View* — /vjuː/ (rhymes with *few*).
- *Upgrade* — /ʌpˈɡreɪd/ (stress on the second syllable for the verb).
- *Should have* — /ˈʃʊd əv/ (often sounds like "should-uv" in fast speech).

## Translation

1 — Hola. Acabo de subir a mi habitación, pero hay un problema.
2 — Siento oír eso, señor. ¿Cuál es el problema?
3 — He notado que el aire acondicionado no funciona y la habitación es bastante ruidosa.
4 — ¿Intentó ajustar el mando en la pared?
5 — Sí, lo intenté, pero no pasó nada.
6 — Además, reservé una habitación que tiene vistas al parque, pero esta da a los cubos de basura.
7 — Déjeme comprobar su reserva en nuestra aplicación... Ah, ya veo el problema.
8 — Ha habido un error en nuestro sistema; deberíamos haberle dado la habitación 402.
9 — ¿Es posible cambiarse ahora? Tengo que trabajar mañana y necesito un espacio tranquilo.
10 — Si espera un momento, comprobaré si el personal de limpieza ha terminado esa habitación.
11 — Sí, está lista. Aquí tiene su nueva tarjeta llave para la 402.
12 — Genial. ¿Tiene esta habitación el wifi de alta velocidad que vi en su web?
13 — Sí, lo tiene. Y le hemos mejorado el desayuno por las molestias.
14 — Gracias, es muy amable de su parte.

## Notes

- **(1)** *I've just gone up*: Use the Present Perfect with *just* for actions that happened very recently. 
- **(2)** *Look onto*: This phrasal verb is used for windows or buildings that face a certain direction. Spanish speakers often incorrectly say "give to".
- **(3)** *Should have + past participle*: This expresses a missed obligation or a mistake in the past.
- **(4)** *Upgrade*: In travel contexts, this means to give a better service or room than what was originally paid for.

## Exercises

1. Change to the Present Perfect: "I book a room" → "I ____ _______ a room."
2. Complete the first conditional: "If you _____ (wait), I ____ (check) the system."
3. Transform using 'should have': "You gave me the wrong key (mistake)." → "You ______ ____ _____ me the right key."
4. Combine with a relative clause: "I saw a website. It had high-speed Wi-Fi." → "The website ____ I saw ___ high-speed Wi-Fi."

**Answer key:** 1 have booked. 2 wait / will check. 3 should have given. 4 that (or which) / had.

## Agent cues

Line 3 and 6 contain the core complaints; ensure the learner uses the Present Perfect (*I've noticed*) and the relative clause (*which has a view*). If the learner says "the room gives to the bins," correct it to *looks onto*. In line 8, emphasize the "shoo-duv" pronunciation for *should have*. Keep the receptionist's tone professional and apologetic, while the guest should be firm but polite.

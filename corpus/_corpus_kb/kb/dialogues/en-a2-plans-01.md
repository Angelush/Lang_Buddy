## Dialogue

1 — Sofia: Hey Leo, are you going to be busy on Saturday night?
2 — Leo: I'm not sure. I can't go out late because I have a big exam on Monday.
3 — Sofia: Oh, that's a shame. There is a jazz concert at the park at 6:00 PM.
4 — Leo: At 6:00? I can do that! Who is playing?
5 — Sofia: A local band. They are better than the one we saw last month.
6 — Leo: Cool. Is it free or do we need to buy tickets on the app?
7 — Sofia: It's free! We just need to bring some snacks.
8 — Leo: Perfect. Let's meet at the park entrance at 5:45.
9 — Sofia: Great. I'm going to invite Mark too.
10 — Leo: Awesome. See you there!

## Pronunciation

- *Busy* — /ˈbɪzi/ (rhymes with "izzy", not "buzzy").
- *Exam* — /ɪɡˈzæm/ (the **x** sounds like **gz**).
- *Shame* — /ʃeɪm/ (long **a** sound).
- *Entrance* — /ˈentrəns/ (stress on the first syllable).
- *Better than* — /ˈbetər ðæn/ (in fast speech, the **t** sounds like a soft **d**).

## Translation

1 — Sofia: Oye Leo, ¿vas a estar ocupado el sábado por la noche?
2 — Leo: No estoy seguro. No puedo salir hasta tarde porque tengo un examen importante el lunes.
3 — Sofia: Oh, qué lástima. Hay un concierto de jazz en el parque a las 6:00 PM.
4 — Leo: ¿A las 6:00? ¡Eso sí puedo hacerlo! ¿Quién toca?
5 — Sofia: Una banda local. Son mejores que la que vimos el mes pasado.
6 — Leo: Genial. ¿Es gratis o tenemos que comprar entradas en la aplicación?
7 — Sofia: ¡Es gratis! Solo tenemos que llevar algo de picar.
8 — Leo: Perfecto. Quedemos en la entrada del parque a las 5:45.
9 — Sofia: Genial. Voy a invitar a Mark también.
10 — Leo: Fantástico. ¡Nos vemos allí!

## Notes

- **(1)** *Are you going to be...?* uses the **be + going to + verb** structure for future plans. In fast speech, *going to* often sounds like "gonna".
- **(2)** *That’s a shame* is a common idiom to express disappointment, similar to "qué lástima" or "qué pena" in Spanish.
- **(3)** *Better than* is the comparative form of *good*. We use *than* (not *that*) for comparisons: "A is better than B."
- **(4)** *Let's meet* is the standard way to suggest a meeting point or time. It is a contraction of *Let us*.

## Exercises

1. Change to the future: "I am busy." → "I .. .. .. busy."
2. Fill in the comparative: "This band is .. (good) than the last one."
3. Choose the correct preposition: "Let's meet .. the entrance .. 5:00."
4. Make it negative: "I can go out late." → "I .. .. out late."

**Answer key:** 1 am going to be. 2 better. 3 at / at. 4 can't go.

## Agent cues

Focus on the distinction between *can* and *can't*—ensure the learner pronounces the /t/ or uses the longer vowel in the negative to avoid confusion. For line 1, anticipate the learner might forget the verb "to be" in the *going to* structure (e.g., "Are you going busy?"). Correct by modeling: "Are you going **to be** busy?". Encourage the use of the contraction "I'm" instead of "I am" to maintain a familiar register. Common ES-L1 error: saying "more better" instead of "better". Correct immediately if this occurs.

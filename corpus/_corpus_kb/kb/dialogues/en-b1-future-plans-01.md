## Dialogue

1 — Hey Chloe, look at this app. I’ve just started a "New Year" list for my ambitions.
2 — Oh no, not again! You used to say that every December and then you did nothing.
3 — This time is different. If I save enough money, I’ll go to Japan next spring.
4 — Japan? That sounds amazing! Have you already booked the flights?
5 — No, not yet. I have to find a better job first so I can afford it.
6 — My current boss, who is quite strict, definitely won’t give me a promotion.
7 — You’re right. You should look for a company that values your skills more.
8 — Exactly. I’ll start sending my CV tomorrow. If I get an interview, will you help me?
9 — Of course! I’ll help you prepare. What else is on your list?
10 — I hope to run a marathon. I’ve already bought some expensive running shoes.
11 — Careful! If you run too much without training, you’ll hurt your knees.
12 — I know. I’ll download a training plan tonight. I’m determined to change my life!

## Pronunciation

- *I’ve just started* — /aɪv dʒʌst ˈstɑːtɪd/ (the **v** in *I’ve* is very light).
- *promotion* — /prəˈməʊʃən/ (stress the second syllable).
- *interview* — /ˈɪntəvjuː/ (in fast speech, the **t** is often silent: "inner-view").
- *determined* — /dɪˈtɜːmɪnd/ (note the **-ed** sounds like a simple **d**).

## Translation

1 — Oye Chloe, mira esta aplicación. Acabo de empezar una lista de "Año Nuevo" para mis ambiciones.
2 — ¡Oh no, otra vez no! Solías decir eso cada diciembre y luego no hacías nada.
3 — Esta vez es diferente. Si ahorro suficiente dinero, iré a Japón la próxima primavera.
4 — ¿Japón? ¡Eso suena increíble! ¿Ya has reservado los vuelos?
5 — No, todavía no. Primero tengo que encontrar un trabajo mejor para poder permitírmelo.
6 — Mi jefe actual, que es bastante estricto, definitivamente no me dará un ascenso.
7 — Tienes razón. Deberías buscar una empresa que valore más tus habilidades.
8 — Exacto. Empezaré a enviar mi CV mañana. Si consigo una entrevista, ¿me ayudarás?
9 — ¡Por supuesto! Te ayudaré a prepararte. ¿Qué más hay en tu lista?
10 — Espero correr un maratón. Ya he comprado unas zapatillas de correr caras.
11 — ¡Cuidado! Si corres demasiado sin entrenar, te harás daño en las rodillas.
12 — Lo sé. Descargaré un plan de entrenamiento esta noche. ¡Estoy decidido a cambiar mi vida!

## Notes

- **(1)** *Used to* is used for past habits that are no longer true. It translates to the Spanish imperfecto (*solía*).
- **(2)** *First Conditional* (*If + present, will + infinitive*): used for real possibilities. Note that we don't use *will* after *if* (*If I save...* NOT *If I will save...*).
- **(3)** *Promotion* vs. *Ascenso*: In a professional context, *promotion* is the standard term for moving up in a company.
- **(4)** *Relative Clauses*: *Who* is used for people (*my boss, who...*) and *that/which* for things or companies (*a company that...*).
- **(5)** *Afford*: A very common B1 verb meaning "tener suficiente dinero para pagar algo".

## Exercises

1. Complete with the correct form: "If I _____ (find) a new job, I _____ (buy) a car."
2. Transform using *used to*: "I played football every day as a child." → "I _____ _____ _____ football every day."
3. Choose the right relative pronoun: "The app _____ I downloaded is great." (who / which)
4. Use *already* or *yet*: "I haven't finished my CV _____, but I have _____ bought a laptop."

**Answer key:** 1 find / will buy. 2 used to play. 3 which (or that). 4 yet / already.

## Agent cues

Focus on the first conditional structure in lines 3, 8, and 11; learners often want to put "will" in the "if" clause. In line 2, check for the correct pronunciation of *used to* (/juːst tə/). When the learner discusses their own plans, encourage the use of *I hope to...* or *I'm planning to...*. Common ES-L1 error: saying "I will help you if I will have time" instead of "if I have time". Correct this by highlighting the "if" block as the present-tense trigger.

## Dialogue

1 — Look at this photo on my phone.
2 — Oh! Is this your family?
3 — Yes. This is my father. His name is David.
4 — He is tall! What is his job?
5 — He is a teacher. And this is my mother, Elena.
6 — She is very pretty. Do you have a brother?
7 — No, I don't. I have two sisters. They are students.
8 — They are young. Is that your dog?
9 — Yes! His name is Sparky. He is part of the family too.

## Pronunciation

- *Photo* — /ˈfoʊtoʊ/ (the **o** sounds are long).
- *Father* — /ˈfɑːðər/ (soft **th** sound).
- *Teacher* — /ˈtiːtʃər/ (stress on the first syllable).
- *Student* — /ˈstjuːdənt/ (notice the "y" sound after the **t** in British English, or /'stu:dənt/ in American).
- *They are* — /ðeɪ ɑːr/ (often links to: "they-are").

## Translation

1 — Mira esta foto en mi teléfono.
2 — ¡Ah! ¿Es esta tu familia?
3 — Sí. Este es mi padre. Su nombre es David.
4 — ¡Es alto! ¿Cuál es su trabajo?
5 — Es profesor. Y esta es mi madre, Elena.
6 — Es muy guapa. ¿Tienes un hermano?
7 — No. Tengo dos hermanas. Son estudiantes.
8 — Son jóvenes. ¿Es ese tu perro?
9 — ¡Sí! Su nombre es Sparky. Él también es parte de la familia.

## Notes

- **(1)** **Possessive Adjectives:** Use *his* for a man (*his name*) and *her* for a woman. *Your* is used for the person you are talking to.
- **(2)** **Articles and Jobs:** In English, we always use *a/an* before a profession: *He is **a** teacher* (not "He is teacher").
- **(3)** **Subject Pronouns:** Unlike Spanish, you must include the subject in English. You cannot just say "Is tall"; you must say "**He** is tall."
- **(4)** **Plurals:** Most nouns just add **-s**: *sister → sisters*, *student → students*.

## Exercises

1. Change to the feminine: "He is a teacher." → "She is a ... ."
2. Use the correct possessive: "This is David. .. name is David." (His / Her)
3. Make a question for this answer: "Yes, I have two sisters." → "Do you ... ... ?"
4. Fill in the gap: "They .. students." (am / is / are)

**Answer key:** 1 teacher. 2 His. 3 have sisters. 4 are.

## Agent cues

Anticipation gaps: line 3 (*His name __ David*), line 5 (*He is __ teacher*), line 7 (*They __ students*). Spanish speakers often forget the "a" in "a teacher" and drop the subject pronouns "He/She/They". Focus on those during correction. Keep the tone warm and helpful, as if looking at a friend's family album. Use a clear, rising intonation for the questions in lines 2, 4, 6, and 8.

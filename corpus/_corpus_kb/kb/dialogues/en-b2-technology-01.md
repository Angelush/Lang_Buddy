## Dialogue

1 — Sofia, are you still answering those emails? We’re supposed to be taking a break.
2 — I know, I know! It’s just that if I didn’t reply now, I’d be swamped tomorrow morning.
3 — I get it, but don't you think being constantly connected is starting to take a toll on our focus?
4 — Well, despite the distractions, I’d say smartphones have made us incredibly efficient. Think of the time we save.
5 — Efficiency is one thing, but it is often argued that our attention spans are being eroded by the constant influx of notifications.
6 — That might be true, although I wouldn't trade the convenience of having the world’s knowledge in my pocket for anything.
7 — Fair enough. However, I read an article recently where a psychologist claimed that we’re losing the art of deep conversation.
8 — Really? He said that? I think it just changes the way we communicate, rather than the quality.
9 — If I had known how addictive these apps were going to be, I might have set stricter boundaries from the start.
10 — We all could have. But let's be honest, could you really get by without your navigation or banking apps?
11 — Probably not. It’s a double-edged sword, isn’t it? Useful tools that eventually start using us.
12 — Precisely. Anyway, I’ll put it away now. I wouldn’t want you to think I’m ignoring you!
13 — Thanks. It’s just that if we don’t disconnect occasionally, we end up feeling totally burnt out.
14 — Point taken. So, what were you saying about that new exhibition?

## Pronunciation

- *Swamped* — /swɒmpt/ (the final **ed** sounds like a sharp **t**).
- *Psychologist* — /saɪˈkɒl.ə.dʒɪst/ (the initial **p** is silent).
- *Eroded* — /ɪˈrəʊ.dɪd/ (ensure the second syllable has the "oh" sound as in "go").
- *Efficiency* — /ɪˈfɪʃ.ən.si/ (the **ci** sounds like "sh").
- *Burnt out* — /ˌbɜːnt ˈaʊt/ (link the **t** to the **o**).

## Translation

1 — Sofía, ¿sigues respondiendo a esos correos? Se supone que nos estamos tomando un descanso.
2 — ¡Lo sé, lo sé! Es solo que si no respondiera ahora, estaría desbordada mañana por la mañana.
3 — Lo entiendo, ¿pero no crees que estar constantemente conectado está empezando a pasar factura a nuestra concentración?
4 — Bueno, a pesar de las distracciones, yo diría que los teléfonos inteligentes nos han hecho increíblemente eficientes. Piensa en el tiempo que ahorramos.
5 — La eficiencia es una cosa, pero a menudo se argumenta que nuestra capacidad de atención se está viendo erosionada por la constante afluencia de notificaciones.
6 — Puede que sea cierto, aunque no cambiaría por nada la comodidad de tener el conocimiento del mundo en mi bolsillo.
7 — Justo. Sin embargo, hace poco leí un artículo donde un psicólogo afirmaba que estamos perdiendo el arte de la conversación profunda.
8 — ¿De verdad? ¿Dijo eso? Creo que solo cambia la forma en que nos comunicamos, más que la calidad.
9 — Si hubiera sabido lo adictivas que iban a ser estas aplicaciones, podría haber establecido límites más estrictos desde el principio.
10 — Todos podríamos haberlo hecho. Pero seamos sinceros, ¿podrías realmente apañártelas sin tus aplicaciones de navegación o de banca?
11 — Probablemente no. Es un arma de doble filo, ¿verdad? Herramientas útiles que al final empiezan a usarnos a nosotros.
12 — Precisamente. De todos modos, lo guardaré ahora. ¡No querría que pensaras que te estoy ignorando!
13 — Gracias. Es solo que si no desconectamos ocasionalmente, terminamos sintiéndonos totalmente quemados.
14 — Tienes razón. Entonces, ¿qué estabas diciendo sobre esa nueva exposición?

## Notes

- **(1)** *Swamped* is a common idiomatic adjective in professional contexts meaning overwhelmed or having too much to deal with.
- **(2)** *To take a toll* is a great B2 phrasal expression used to describe the gradual negative effect or damage caused by something over time.
- **(3)** *Eroded* (Line 5) is our "stretch" vocabulary item here. While usually used for soil or rocks, in a B2/C1 context, it refers to the gradual destruction or weakening of an abstract concept like "trust" or "attention."
- **(4)** The Third Conditional (Line 9: *If I had known... I might have set...*) is used to talk about hypothetical past situations and their possible outcomes.
- **(5)** *Point taken* is a neutral, slightly formal way to say "I accept your argument" or "I understand what you mean."

## Exercises

1. Complete the second conditional: "If I _____ (have) more self-control, I _____ (delete) social media."
2. Vocabulary match: Which word means "to survive or manage with limited resources"? (a) Swamped (b) Get by (c) Burnt out.
3. Rewrite using the passive voice: "Constant notifications are eroding our focus." -> "Our focus ______ ______ ______ by constant notifications."
4. Use a discourse marker: "I love my smartphone; ________, I worry about my privacy." (However / Despite).

**Answer key:** 1. had / would delete. 2. (b) Get by. 3. is being eroded. 4. However.

## Dialogue

1 — So, Maya, have you finally reached a decision about that fintech offer?
2 — Honestly, Leo, I’m still torn. If the salary were the only factor, I’d have signed the contract yesterday.
3 — But it isn’t, right? You’ve always valued your work-life balance above everything else.
4 — Exactly. Although the perks are incredible, I’d be expected to be on call quite often.
5 — That sounds exhausting. Didn’t you say you were told the role was fully remote?
6 — It is, mostly. However, the manager hinted that I might need to fly out to Berlin once a month.
7 — Well, if I were in your shoes, I’d weigh the long-term prospects. Is there room for growth?
8 — Definitely. I’ve been passed over for promotion twice at my current firm, which is why this is so tempting.
9 — Despite the risks, it seems like a leap worth taking. You don't want to look back and wonder "what if."
10 — True. My partner also pointed out that I’d regret turning it down if the company goes public.
11 — Still, I need to sleep on it. I'll probably get back to them by Friday.
12 — Just make sure you aren't being talked into something that doesn't align with your goals.
13 — Thanks, Leo. I'll keep you posted once I've made up my mind.

## Translation

1 — Entonces, Maya, ¿has tomado finalmente una decisión sobre esa oferta de la fintech?
2 — Sinceramente, Leo, todavía estoy dividida. Si el salario fuera el único factor, habría firmado el contrato ayer.
3 — Pero no lo es, ¿verdad? Siempre has valorado tu equilibrio entre vida laboral y personal por encima de todo.
4 — Exactamente. Aunque los beneficios son increíbles, se esperaría que estuviera de guardia con bastante frecuencia.
5 — Eso suena agotador. ¿No dijiste que te habían dicho que el puesto era totalmente remoto?
6 — Lo es, en su mayor parte. Sin embargo, el gerente insinuó que podría necesitar viajar a Berlín una vez al mes.
7 — Bueno, si yo estuviera en tu lugar, sopesaría las perspectivas a largo plazo. ¿Hay margen de crecimiento?
8 — Definitivamente. Me han pasado por alto para un ascenso dos veces en mi empresa actual, por eso esto es tan tentador.
9 — A pesar de los riesgos, parece un salto que vale la pena dar. No querrás mirar atrás y preguntarte "qué hubiera pasado si".
10 — Cierto. Mi pareja también señaló que me arrepentiría de rechazarlo si la empresa sale a bolsa.
11 — Aun así, necesito consultarlo con la almohada. Probablemente les responderé para el viernes.
12 — Solo asegúrate de que no te estén convenciendo de algo que no se alinee con tus objetivos.
13 — Gracias, Leo. Te mantendré informado en cuanto me haya decidido.

## Notes

- **(1)** *I'm torn* is a common idiom meaning to be undecided between two choices, as if being pulled in two directions.
- **(2)** The *Second Conditional* ("If I were...") is used here for hypothetical situations. In B2 English, we prefer *were* over *was* for all persons in the "if" clause.
- **(3)** *On call* refers to being available to work if needed, even outside of standard hours, common in tech and medicine.
- **(4)** *Passed over for promotion* is a specific passive structure used when someone else gets a job you were qualified for.
- **(5)** *Sleep on it* is a common phrasal verb meaning to delay a decision until the next day to have more time to think.

## Exercises

1. Complete with the correct form: "If I _____ (be) you, I would take the job."
2. Change to the passive voice: "They expect me to work late." → "I ____ ________ to work late."
3. Which discourse marker fits? "_______ the high salary, she declined the offer." (Despite / Although)
4. Use a phrasal verb to replace the bracketed words: "I decided to [reject] ________ the offer."
5. What does "to go public" mean for a company?

**Answer key:** 1 were. 2 am expected. 3 Despite. 4 turn down. 5 To start selling shares on the stock market.

## Agent cues

Focus on the contrast between the hypothetical *would* and the current reality. If the learner says "If I was," acknowledge it's common in casual speech but prompt for "were" for more formal or exam-style accuracy. Pay attention to the linking in phrasal verbs like *fly out* and *turn it down*. For line 9, ensure the learner uses the gerund *taking* after *worth*. Common L1-Spanish error: saying "I have been passed" instead of "I have been passed over."

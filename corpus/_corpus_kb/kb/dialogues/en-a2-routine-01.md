## Dialogue

1 — Hi Mia! You're late. Did you oversleep?
2 — No, I didn't. I usually wake up at 7:00, but my fitness app didn't work.
3 — That's a bad start! Did you go to the gym anyway?
4 — Yes, I went for a quick run. Then I made a smoothie and started work.
5 — I was very busy yesterday. I worked until 8:00 PM.
6 — That's very late! What did you do after that?
7 — I watched a movie and then I went to bed. I was exhausted.
8 — Well, are you going to do anything fun tonight?
9 — I'm going to meet some friends for pizza. Do you want to come?
10 — I can't. I'm going to stay home and relax tonight.

## Pronunciation
- Usually — /ˈjuːʒuəli/ (three syllables: "yoo-zhoo-lee").
- Watched — /wɒtʃt/ (the 'ed' sounds like a sharp 't').
- Exhausted — /ɪɡˈzɔːstɪd/ (the 'h' is silent; stress on the second syllable).
- Fitness app — /ˈfɪtnəs æp/ (link the 's' of fitness to the 'a' of app).

## Translation
1 — ¡Hola Mia! Llegas tarde. ¿Te quedaste dormida?
2 — No. Normalmente me despierto a las 7:00, pero mi aplicación de fitness no funcionó.
3 — ¡Vaya comienzo! ¿Fuiste al gimnasio de todos modos?
4 — Sí, fui a correr un poco. Luego preparé un batido y empecé a trabajar.
5 — Ayer estuve muy ocupado. Trabajé hasta las 8:00 p. m.
6 — ¡Eso es muy tarde! ¿Qué hiciste después de eso?
7 — Vi una película y luego me fui a la cama. Estaba agotado.
8 — Bueno, ¿vas a hacer algo divertido esta noche?
9 — Voy a quedar con unos amigos para comer pizza. ¿Quieres venir?
10 — No puedo. Me voy a quedar en casa y relajarme esta noche.

## Notes
- (1) **Oversleep**: to sleep longer than you intended. Different from "sleep in" (which is intentional, on purpose).
- (2) **Didn't work**: the past negative of every verb except *to be* uses *did not (didn't)* + the base form. The main verb *work* stays unchanged.
- (3) **Anyway**: here it means "despite the problem with the app."
- (7) **Exhausted**: a stronger word for "very, very tired." A stretch word for A2, but very common in daily life.
- (8) **Going to**: used for plans and intentions already decided before speaking.

## Exercises
1. Complete with the past simple: "Yesterday, I .. (go) to the park and I .. (watch) the birds."
2. Fill in the missing word for the future plan: "We .. going to play video games tonight."
3. Transform to negative: "I woke up at 6:00." -> "I .. .. up at 6:00."
4. Use a frequency adverb (100% of the time): "I .. drink coffee in the morning."

**Answer key:** 1 went / watched. 2 are. 3 didn't wake. 4 always.

## Agent cues
Anticipation gaps: line 2 (*didn't* + base verb), line 8 (the *going to* question), line 9 (stating an intention). Model the contrast between the sharp "t" ending of *watched* and the "id" ending of *exhausted*. Keep a friendly, conversational pace. Watch for the common ES-L1 error of conjugating the verb after *didn't* ("didn't went").

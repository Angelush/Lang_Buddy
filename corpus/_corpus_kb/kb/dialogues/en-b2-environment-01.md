## Dialogue

1 — Leo: Have you seen that new app that calculates your carbon footprint based on your daily commute?
2 — Clara: I've heard of it, but if I didn't drive, I'd never get the kids to school on time.
3 — Leo: Although it's a bit of a juggle, the new cycle lanes were specifically designed to make it safer for families.
4 — Clara: I was told that they're often blocked by delivery vans, though.
5 — Leo: That's being addressed by the council now. New cameras are being installed this week.
6 — Clara: Still, despite the improvements, I'm not sure I'm ready to give up my car just yet.
7 — Leo: If you had switched to an e-bike last year, you would have saved a lot on petrol by now.
8 — Clara: Perhaps, but think of the grocery shopping! It can't be carried on a bike easily.
9 — Leo: Actually, most supermarkets offer free delivery if you spend over fifty pounds.
10 — Clara: You've thought of everything, haven't you?
11 — Leo: I'm just saying that if we all made small adjustments, the collective impact would be significant.
12 — Clara: I suppose I could try it on Fridays. It's better than doing nothing, I guess.

- *Although it's* — /ɔːlˈðəʊ ɪts/ (the "th" is voiced /ð/ as in "then").

## Translation

1 — Leo: ¿Has visto esa nueva aplicación que calcula tu huella de carbono según tu trayecto diario al trabajo?
2 — Clara: He oído hablar de ella, pero si no condujera, nunca llevaría a los niños al colegio a tiempo.
3 — Leo: Aunque es un poco difícil de compaginar, los nuevos carriles bici se diseñaron específicamente para que sean más seguros para las familias.
4 — Clara: Me dijeron que a menudo están bloqueados por furgonetas de reparto, sin embargo.
5 — Leo: El ayuntamiento está abordando eso ahora. Se están instalando cámaras nuevas esta semana.
6 — Clara: Aun así, a pesar de las mejoras, no estoy segura de estar lista para renunciar a mi coche todavía.
7 — Leo: Si te hubieras pasado a una bicicleta eléctrica el año pasado, habrías ahorrado mucho en gasolina para estas alturas.
8 — Clara: Quizás, ¡pero piensa en la compra! No se puede llevar en una bici fácilmente.
9 — Leo: De hecho, la mayoría de los supermercados ofrecen entrega gratuita si gastas más de cincuenta libras.
10 — Clara: Has pensado en todo, ¿verdad?
11 — Leo: Solo digo que si todos hiciéramos pequeños ajustes, el impacto colectivo sería significativo.
12 — Clara: Supongo que podría probar los viernes. Es mejor que no hacer nada, supongo.

## Notes

- **(1)** **Second vs Third Conditional**: Line 2 (*If I didn't drive...*) refers to a present/general habit, while Line 7 (*If you had switched...*) refers to a past opportunity that was missed.
- **(2)** **Passive Voice**: *...are being installed* (present continuous passive) and *...were specifically designed* (past simple passive) shift the focus from who did the action to what is happening.
- **(3)** **Discourse Markers**: *Although* is followed by a clause (*it's a bit of a juggle*), whereas *despite* is followed by a noun phrase (*the improvements*).
- **(4)** **A bit of a juggle**: An idiomatic way to describe managing several difficult tasks or responsibilities at the same time.

## Exercises

1. Change this to the passive voice: "The council is installing new cameras this week."
2. Complete the third conditional: "If I _______ (know) about the traffic, I _______ (take) the train."
3. Choose the correct word: "________ (Although / Despite) the rain, he cycled to work."
4. Rewrite using the second conditional: "I don't have an e-bike, so I drive to work." (If I...)

**Answer key:** 1. New cameras are being installed by the council this week. 2. had known / would have taken. 3. Despite. 4. If I had an e-bike, I wouldn't drive to work.

## Patterns

- [[patterns/if-clauses]] — Using *if + past* for present imaginary situations and *if + past perfect* for past regrets.
- [[patterns/passive-monitoring]] — Using the passive voice to describe urban management (*being addressed*, *being installed*).
- [[patterns/although-despite]] — Contradicting expectations using different grammatical structures.

## Agent cues

Focus on the contrast between Leo’s encouraging tone and Clara’s initial skepticism. In line 7, ensure the learner pronounces the weak "have" in "would have saved". If the learner says "Despite it is improved", correct them to "Despite the improvement" or "Although it is improved". Pay attention to the passive voice in line 5; learners often forget the "being" in the present continuous passive.

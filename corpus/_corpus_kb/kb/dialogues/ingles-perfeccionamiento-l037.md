## Lesson notes
- **Soft drink**: non-alcoholic beverage; opposite is *alcoholic drink*. In US: *hard liquor* for strongly alcoholized drinks (whisky, bourbon...). Note: asking for *whisky* in GB gets you *scotch*; in US, *bourbon*.
- **Terrible**: very bad. **Flop**: failure, disaster. *His latest film was a flop* = Su última película fue una birria. (Note: in US *His latest film bombed*, but Brits would take this as a compliment, since *bomb* in GB means *triumphed*!).
- **Album**: for several years now = record album (33 rpm), but *photo album* still used.
- **Why bother / Don’t bother!** = leave it, why do it? — *Let me explain* — *Don’t bother, it’s too late* = No merece la pena, es demasiado tarde. **Don’t bother him, he’s busy** = No lo moleste, está ocupado.
- **Software**: frequently used for a computer’s operating system + programs that translate high-level languages into machine language. Opposite = *hardware*. A *software package*.
- **Inner city**: increasingly used in GB and US; means the center of an urban area, the most economically deprived and abandoned part.
- **Are you familiar with...?** = slightly elaborate way of saying *Do you know...?*. More common in American English.
- **Sore**: very useful adj. *Ooh! my back’s sore after all that gardening* = ¡Dios, me duele la espalda después de todo ese trabajo de jardinería! **Eyesore** = something ugly to look at.
- **To upgrade**: to improve (literally, move to a higher grade). Often used for systems. *The system was upgraded to meet new standards* = El sistema se mejoró para satisfacer las nuevas normas.
- **Mess**: disorder. *Your room is a mess. Clear it up!* = Tu habitación es una verdadera leonera. ¡Arréglala! *I’m afraid we’re in a terrible mess* = ¡Vaya lío!
- **A load**: a cargo. Here used colloquially = a lot of. *I’ve got a load of things to do* = Tengo un montón de cosas que hacer.

2. — I’ll call him at home. — Don’t bother; he left for the weekend.
3. — Are you familiar with this author? — [I’ve never heard of him, I’m afraid.
4. My ankle was sore for a week after the fall.

1. ¿Tendré que comprarme una camisa nueva? — ¿Por qué preocuparse? Esa está perfecta. → — Shall I buy a new shirt? —... ...... ? That one’s fine.

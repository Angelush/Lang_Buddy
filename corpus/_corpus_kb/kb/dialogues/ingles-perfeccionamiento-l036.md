## Lesson notes
- **Rosy**: adj. for rose flower; *to be rosy* = going well, in good condition. Note phrase: *...is far from (being) perfect* = está lejos de ser perfecto.
- **Peak**: n. mountain top; *to peak* (journalism) = reach maximum. *The share price peaked at $15* = El precio de las acciones alcanzó un máximo de $15.
- **GNP/GDP**: Gross National Product / Gross Domestic Product (producto nacional bruto / producto interior bruto).
- **Slug/Sluggish**: n. snail; adj. slow, lethargic. *The engine is sluggish* = El motor es débil.
- **Spree**: binge, frenzy. *When he received his exam results, they all went out on a spree* = se fueron todos de juerga.
- **Curb**: n. (US: Kerb) edge of sidewalk; *to curb* = restrain, reduce. *The government is attempting to curb consumer spending* = El gobierno intenta restringir los gastos de consumo.
- **Somewhat**: elegant adj. meaning *a little*. More common: *rather*. *It’s somewhat difficult to explain* = Es un poco difícil explicárselo.
- **Fuel**: n. gasoline; *to fuel* (figurative) = add to. *His latest book has fueled rumours that he is divorced* = Su último libro ha alimentado los rumores sobre su divorcio.
- **Plant**: n. factory; macroeconomic *plant* = industrial infrastructure. *The country has to renew its aging plant* = El país está obligado a renovar su estructura industrial antigua.
- **Tumble**: v. to fall rapidly (figurative). *Prices tumbled after Christmas* = Los precios han sucumbido pasada la Navidad.
- **Doldrums**: n. (nautical) calm zone; (journalism) economic slump. *Britain’s economy was in the doldrums* = La economía británica estaba en un marasmo.
- **Bottom out**: v. reach lowest level (and stop falling). *The unemployment rate has bottomed out at 3%* = La tasa de desempleo ha alcanzado su nivel más bajo en el 3%. Adverbial phrase is obligatory.

## Exercises
**Sentence transformation:**
1. Unemployment peaked at twelve percent of the active population
2. Two important economic indicators are the gross national product (GNP) and the gross domestic product (GDP).
3. The economy is sluggish and new investments are desperately needed.
4. When he received his first pay packet he went on a shopping spree... and spent everything.
5. The law is an attempt to curb the influence of the trade unions.
6. — What fuel do you use in that engine? — Diesel oil.

**Fill in the missing words:**
1. Su empresa está prácticamente paralizada. → His company is really... ... ..........
2. La inflación por fin ha tocado fondo. → Inflation ... at last ........
3. La economía es una ciencia bastante complicada. → Economics... . cc. .... complicated science.
4. La enfermedad del Presidente ha alimentado los rumores de que puede dimitir. → The President’s illness ... ....... ....... that he may resign.
5. El desempleo ha evolucionado hasta su más alto nivel después de la guerra. → Unemployment soared... since

**Answers:** 1. in the doldrums 2. has...bottomed out 3. is a somewhat 4. has fuelled rumours 5. to its highest level...since

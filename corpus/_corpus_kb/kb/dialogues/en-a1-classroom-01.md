## Dialogue

1 — Good morning! Are you the teacher?
2 — Yes, I am. I'm Mrs. Miller. Welcome!
3 — Thank you. My name is Leo. Where is my seat?
4 — You are here, next to the window.
5 — Thanks. Which page is the lesson?
6 — It's page nine in the school app.
7 — Sorry, can you repeat that?
8 — Page nine. Do you have your tablet?
9 — Yes, I do. Thank you, Mrs. Miller.

## Pronunciation

- *Teacher* — /ˈtiːtʃər/ (the **er** is a soft schwa /ə/).
- *Welcome* — /ˈwelkəm/ (stress on the first syllable).
- *Next to the* — /nekst tə ðə/ (the **t** in *next* often blends with *to*).
- *Repeat* — /rɪˈpiːt/ (long **ee** sound).
- *Tablet* — /ˈtæblət/ (the **a** is like in *cat*).

## Translation

1 — ¡Buenos días! ¿Es usted la profesora?
2 — Sí, lo soy. Soy la Sra. Miller. ¡Bienvenido!
3 — Gracias. Mi nombre es Leo. ¿Dónde está mi asiento?
4 — Estás aquí, al lado de la ventana.
5 — Gracias. ¿En qué página está la lección?
6 — Es la página nueve en la aplicación de la escuela.
7 — Perdón, ¿puede repetir eso?
8 — Página nueve. ¿Tienes tu tablet?
9 — Sí, la tengo. Gracias, Sra. Miller.

## Notes

- **(1)** *Mrs.* (pronounced /ˈmɪsɪz/) is used for a married woman. In a classroom
  setting, students use the title + last name (*Mrs. Miller*) to show respect.
- **(2)** *Next to* is a common preposition of place. In English, we say
  *next to the window*, whereas Spanish uses *al lado de*.
- **(3)** *Can you repeat that?* is our stretch item. While *can* for ability is 
  A2, this specific phrase is taught early as a "functional" chunk for survival.
- **(4)** *Which* is used when there is a limited choice (like pages in a book
  or app), similar to *cuál* or *qué*.

## Exercises

1. Complete the question: "______ is the teacher?" — "She is in the classroom."
2. Fill in the possessive: "Leo, where is ____ (you) tablet?"
3. Reorder: repeat / you / that / can / ?
4. Translate: "Al lado de la ventana." → "Next to the ______."

**Answer key:** 1 Where. 2 your. 3 Can you repeat that? 4 window.

## Agent cues

Anticipation gaps: line 3 (*Where is my __?*), line 7 (the polite request for
repetition). If the learner says "where is seat?" instead of "my seat", gently
remind them to use the possessive. Ensure the learner pronounces the /h/ in
*here* and the /w/ in *window* clearly. Register is polite but welcoming; use
a slightly rising intonation for the questions in lines 1, 3, 5, 7, and 8.

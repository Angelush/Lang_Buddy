## Dialogue

1 — So, what did you think of the finale? I thought it was amazing!
2 — Really? I’m sorry, but I didn't like it at all. It was so slow.
3 — Slow? The special effects were the best I’ve seen this year!
4 — Maybe, but the plot was so confusing that I almost fell asleep.
5 — It wasn't confusing if you paid attention to the details in season one.
6 — I used to like sci-fi, but this show has too many characters who don't matter.
7 — But the main actor, who played the captain, was incredible.
8 — He was okay, but he should have explained his plan earlier.
9 — If they make a second season, I’m sure everything will be clearer.
10 — I won't watch it. I have to find something more exciting to stream next weekend.
11 — You're so hard to please! Let's just watch a comedy next time.
12 — That sounds like a plan. We’ll definitely agree on that!

## Translation

1 — Entonces, ¿qué te pareció el final? ¡Me pareció increíble!
2 — ¿En serio? Lo siento, pero no me gustó nada. Fue tan lento.
3 — ¿Lento? ¡Los efectos especiales fueron los mejores que he visto este año!
4 — Quizás, pero la trama era tan confusa que casi me quedo dormida.
5 — No era confusa si prestabas atención a los detalles de la primera temporada.
6 — Antes me gustaba la ciencia ficción, pero esta serie tiene demasiados personajes que no importan.
7 — Pero el actor principal, el que hacía de capitán, estuvo increíble.
8 — Estuvo bien, pero debería haber explicado su plan antes.
9 — Si hacen una segunda temporada, estoy seguro de que todo estará más claro.
10 — Yo no la veré. Tengo que encontrar algo más emocionante para ver en streaming el próximo fin de semana.
11 — ¡Eres tan difícil de complacer! Simplemente veamos una comedia la próxima vez.
12 — Me parece un buen plan. ¡En eso seguro que nos ponemos de acuerdo!

## Notes

- **(1)** *Finale* refers specifically to the last episode of a season or series. For a movie, we just say "the ending".
- **(2)** *I used to like* (Antes me gustaba) describes a finished habit or state in the past that is no longer true.
- **(3)** *Relative clauses*: "Characters **who** don't matter". We use **who** for people and **which** or **that** for things.
- **(4)** *Hard to please*: An idiomatic expression for someone who is very critical or difficult to satisfy.
- **(5)** *Should have explained*: This is a B2 "stretch item". It expresses a past regret or a missed obligation (debería haber...).

## Agent cues

Anticipation prompts: Line 1 (*What ___ you think*), Line 6 (*I ____ to like*), Line 9 (*If they ____, everything ____ be*). 
Correction strategy: If the student uses "I am agree," correct to "I agree." If they forget the "to" in "used to," repeat the phrase with emphasis. 
Prosody: Leo should sound enthusiastic; Clara should sound slightly bored or skeptical. 
Common ES-L1 error: Using "the" before "sci-fi" (English often drops the article for genres) and confusing "boring" with "bored" (e.g., "the film was bored" instead of "boring").

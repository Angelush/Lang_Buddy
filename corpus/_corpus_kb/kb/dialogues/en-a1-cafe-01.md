## Dialogue

1 — Hello! A coffee, please.
2 — Small or large? — Large, please.
3 — Anything else? — A croissant, please.
4 — Is that all? — Yes, thank you. How much is it?
5 — It's eight pounds fifty.
6 — Do you have a card? — No, I have the app on my phone.
7 — That's perfect. Scan here, please.
8 — Okay. Thank you! — You're welcome.

## Pronunciation

- *Coffee* — /ˈkɒfi/ (short 'o' sound).
- *Croissant* — /ˈkrwʌsɒ̃/ (the 't' is silent).
- *Eight pounds fifty* — /eɪt paʊndz ˈfɪf.ti/ (link 'pounds' and 'fifty').
- *How much is it?* — /haʊ mʌtʃ ɪz ɪt/ (the words link together: "mu-chi-zit").
- *Scan* — /skæn/ (short 'a' as in *cat*).

## Translation

1 — ¡Hola! Un café, por favor.
2 — ¿Pequeño o grande? — Grande, por favor.
3 — ¿Algo más? — Un cruasán, por favor.
4 — ¿Eso es todo? — Sí, gracias. ¿Cuánto es?
5 — Son ocho libras cincuenta.
6 — ¿Tienes tarjeta? — No, tengo la aplicación en mi teléfono.
7 — Perfecto. Escanee aquí, por favor.
8 — Vale. ¡Gracias! — De nada.

## Notes

- **(1)** *Anything else?* is the standard short way to ask if the customer wants 
  more items. In Spanish, we say "¿Algo más?".
- **(2)** *How much is it?* is the essential phrase for prices. Note the use of 
  *it* at the end; Spanish speakers often forget this and just say "How much is?".
- **(3)** *Pounds* (£) is the currency of the UK. Prices like *eight pounds fifty* 
  mean £8.50. You don't need to say the word "pence" at the end.
- **(4)** *You're welcome* is the most common response to *thank you*. It is the 
  equivalent of "de nada".

## Exercises

1. Complete the price question: "How ____ is it?"
2. Use *a* or *an*: ".. coffee" / ".. app".
3. Reorder: large / or / small / ?
4. Complete with *have*: "I .. the app on my phone."

**Answer key:** 1 much. 2 a / an. 3 Small or large? 4 have.

## Agent cues

Anticipation prompts: Line 4 (*How ___ is it?*), Line 6 (*I ___ the app*), and Line 8 (*You're ____*). 
Common ES-L1 error: ensure the learner uses "it" in the price question; if they say "How much is?", 
prompt with "How much is... it?". Register is polite but efficient (busy cafe vibe). 
Pace should be steady, emphasizing the 's' in *pounds* and the 'n' in *scan*.

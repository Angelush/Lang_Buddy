## Dialogue

1 — Sam: Is that your cousin in the photo?
2 — Chloe: No, that's my friend Leo. He's going to come to the party tonight.
3 — Sam: Oh! What does he look like? He looks quite tall.
4 — Chloe: He is! He's taller than me and he has short, dark hair.
5 — Sam: He looks very friendly. Is he nice?
6 — Chloe: He's great! He's very funny and he always tells jokes.
7 — Sam: What is he wearing in this picture?
8 — Chloe: A blue sweater and jeans. He usually wears casual clothes.
9 — Sam: Well, I'm going to meet him later.
10 — Chloe: You can't miss him; he's the one with the big, green glasses!

## Pronunciation

- *What does he look like?* — /wɒt dəz hi lʊk laɪk/ (the words flow together: "wot-duz-ee").
- *Taller than* — /ˈtɔːlər ðæn/ (the **r** in *taller* links to *than*).
- *Clothes* — /kləʊðz/ (careful: it is one syllable, the **e** is silent).
- *Wearing* — /ˈwɛərɪŋ/ (rhymes with *pairing*).

## Translation

1 — Sam: ¿Es ese tu primo en la foto?
2 — Chloe: No, es mi amigo Leo. Va a venir a la fiesta esta noche.
3 — Sam: ¡Ah! ¿Cómo es (físicamente)? Parece bastante alto.
4 — Chloe: ¡Lo es! Es más alto que yo y tiene el pelo corto y oscuro.
5 — Sam: Parece muy simpático. ¿Es majo?
6 — Chloe: ¡Es genial! Es muy divertido y siempre cuenta chistes.
7 — Sam: ¿Qué lleva puesto en esta foto?
8 — Chloe: Un jersey azul y vaqueros. Suele llevar ropa informal.
9 — Sam: Bueno, lo voy a conocer luego.
10 — Chloe: No tiene pérdida; ¡es el de las gafas verdes grandes!

## Notes

- **(1)** *What does he look like?* asks about physical appearance. To ask about personality, we use *What is he like?*.
- **(2)** *Taller than*: In English, we add **-er** to short adjectives to compare. We use *than* (not *that*) for "que".
- **(3)** *Wearing* vs *Dresses*: We use the present continuous (*he is wearing*) for what someone has on right now or in a photo. *Dresses* is for the general habit.
- **(4)** *Funny* vs *Fun*: *Funny* means someone makes you laugh (divertido/chistoso). *Fun* means an activity is enjoyable.

## Exercises

1. Complete the comparative: "Leo is .. (tall) than Chloe."
2. Change to the negative: "He is going to come." → "He .. going to come."
3. Place the adverb: "He tells jokes." (always)
4. Answer the question: "What does Leo look like?" (Mention his hair).

**Answer key:** 1 taller. 2 isn't / is not. 3 He always tells jokes. 4 He has short, dark hair.

## Agent cues

Line 3 is a key anticipation gap: Sam should ask for the physical description using the "look like" pattern. Watch for the common ES-L1 error "How is he?" when asking for appearance; redirect to "What does he look like?". Ensure the learner uses the comparative "taller than" correctly in line 4. In line 10, the phrase "You can't miss him" is an idiomatic stretch—if the learner is confused, explain it means he is very easy to find. Keep the register informal and enthusiastic.

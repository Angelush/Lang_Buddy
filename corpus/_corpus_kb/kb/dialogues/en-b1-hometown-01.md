## Dialogue

1 — Look at this photo on my phone from ten years ago. This street looks so different!
2 — Oh, that's North Street. There used to be a small cinema there, didn't there?
3 — Exactly! It's the place where I saw my first movie. Now it's a huge gym.
4 — I know. The city council built it last year to encourage people to exercise.
5 — And what happened to the park that was behind the library?
6 — They've turned it into a tech hub. There are lots of startups which opened recently.
7 — It's a bit sad. I used to spend all my summer afternoons there with my friends.
8 — True, but now there are more jobs for young people who want to stay here.
9 — That's a good point. I should probably use this map app to find the new park.
10 — You have to try the one near the river. It's much bigger than the old one.
11 — I'll go there tomorrow. I hope it still has those old oak trees.
12 — Yes, they kept those. They're part of the heritage trail now.

## Pronunciation

- *Used to* — /ˈjuːst tə/ (The **d** is silent; it sounds like "yoost-ta").
- *Cinema* — /ˈsɪnəmə/ (Three syllables, stress on the first).
- *Huge* — /hjuːdʒ/ (Start with a strong **h** sound, like a sigh).
- *Heritage* — /ˈherɪtɪdʒ/ (The **g** sounds like the **j** in "jump").
- *Startups which* — /ˈstɑːrtʌps wɪtʃ/ (Link the **s** of *startups* to the **w**).

## Translation

1 — Mira esta foto en mi móvil de hace diez años. ¡Esta calle parece tan diferente!
2 — Oh, esa es la calle Norte. Solía haber un cine pequeño allí, ¿verdad?
3 — ¡Exacto! Es el lugar donde vi mi primera película. Ahora es un gimnasio enorme.
4 — Lo sé. El ayuntamiento lo construyó el año pasado para animar a la gente a hacer ejercicio.
5 — ¿Y qué pasó con el parque que estaba detrás de la biblioteca?
6 — Lo han convertido en un centro tecnológico. Hay muchas empresas emergentes que abrieron recientemente.
7 — Es un poco triste. Yo solía pasar todas mis tardes de verano allí con mis amigos.
8 — Cierto, pero ahora hay más trabajos para los jóvenes que quieren quedarse aquí.
9 — Buen punto. Probablemente debería usar esta aplicación de mapas para encontrar el parque nuevo.
10 — Tienes que probar el que está cerca del río. Es mucho más grande que el antiguo.
11 — Iré allí mañana. Espero que todavía tenga esos viejos robles.
12 — Sí, esos los mantuvieron. Ahora son parte de la ruta del patrimonio.

## Notes

- **(1)** **Used to** is used for past habits or states that are no longer true. Unlike Spanish *solía*, we don't use it for present habits (for those, we use the present simple).
- **(2)** **Relative Clauses**: Use *who* for people ("people who want") and *which* or *that* for things ("startups which opened"). In informal English, *that* is very common for both.
- **(3)** **Huge** is a "strong" adjective meaning "very big." You don't usually say "very huge"; just "huge" or "absolutely huge."
- **(4)** **Should vs. Have to**: *Should* is for advice or suggestions (Line 9), while *Have to* expresses a stronger recommendation or necessity (Line 10).

## Exercises

1. Rewrite using *used to*: "I lived in London, but now I live in Paris." (I ______ in London).
2. Combine the sentences: "I found a map app. It shows the new park." (I found a map app ______ shows the new park).
3. Choose the correct pronoun: "The woman ______ lives next door is an architect." (who / which).
4. Fill in the gap: "There ______ be a library here, but now it's a café."

**Answer key:** 1 used to live. 2 which/that. 3 who. 4 used to.

## Agent cues

Line 2 and 7 are key for checking the pronunciation of *used to*; ensure the learner doesn't pronounce the "d" as a separate syllable. In line 6, watch for the relative pronoun *which*—Spanish speakers often substitute it with *what*. If the learner says "startups what opened," correct it to "startups that" or "which." The register is familiar and nostalgic; encourage a slightly wistful tone in line 7 and an encouraging tone in line 10. For the word *huge*, ensure they don't say "yuge" (dropping the H).

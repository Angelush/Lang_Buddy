# CEFR objective spine — English & French

The authoring backbone. Every original dialogue is written to hit objectives
**defined here**, which derive from the public CEFR framework (`cefr_can_do.md`)
and frequency facts (`freq_anchors.md`) — **never** from any source course's
text. This file is the only "what to teach" input the generators receive.

CEFR is the **primary** axis (mandatory tag); theme is secondary. Levels map to
the Assimil-style spiral arc (recurring vocab, growing structures) but are
graded against CEFR can-do statements, not any book's lesson order.

Legend: **slice** = the two themes built in the current thin-slice pass.
`lines` = target dialogue length. Vocab lists are *representative anchors*, not
closed sets — generators expand within the level's frequency band.

## English (target_lang: en, base_lang: es)

### A1 — `lines: 6–10`, present tense, 4–8 words/line, very slow register
- **Can-do:** introduce self, ask/answer personal details, name everyday things.
- **Grammar focus:** `to be`/`to have` present, articles (a/an/the), plurals,
  subject pronouns, possessive adjectives, yes/no + wh- questions, `isn't/aren't`.
- **Vocab anchors:** greetings, name, family, numbers 1–20, days, common food/drink.
- **Themes:** greetings/introductions **(slice)**, café & ordering **(slice)**,
  family, numbers & time, the classroom, shopping basics.

### A2 — `lines: 8–12`, simple past & near future, connectors and/but/because
- **Can-do:** describe routine & past events, make plans, handle short exchanges.
- **Grammar focus:** past simple (reg/irreg), `going to` future, `can/can't`,
  comparatives, frequency adverbs, prepositions of place/time, `there is/are`.
- **Vocab anchors:** daily routine, weekend, directions, jobs, weather, health.
- **Themes:** daily routine **(slice)**, asking directions **(slice)**,
  last weekend, making plans, at the doctor, describing people.

### B1 — `lines: 10–14`, one subordinate clause, natural unhurried register
- **Can-do:** narrate experiences, give opinions, deal with travel situations.
- **Grammar focus:** present perfect vs past simple, `will` future, 1st conditional,
  relative clauses (who/which/that), modals (should/have to), `used to`.
- **Vocab anchors:** travel, opinions, feelings, problems/complaints, work life.
- **Themes:** a trip you took **(slice)**, opinion on a film/show **(slice)**,
  a problem at a hotel, future plans, describing a hometown.

### B2 — `lines: 12–16`, complex subordination, conditionals, near-normal pace
- **Can-do:** discuss abstract topics, weigh pros/cons, express nuanced opinion.
- **Grammar focus:** 2nd & 3rd conditional, passive voice, reported speech,
  discourse markers (however, although, despite), common phrasal verbs.
- **Vocab anchors:** technology, society, environment, careers, media, abstract qualities.
- **Themes:** technology & daily life — pros/cons **(slice)**, a workplace
  disagreement **(slice)**, news & current affairs, the environment, a career choice.

### C1 — `lines: 14–18`, fully complex, register-aware, idiomatic
- **Can-do:** argue a case, shift register, use idiom precisely.
- **Grammar focus:** inversion, cleft sentences, nuanced modality, advanced
  connectors, idiomatic phrasal verbs, hedging & emphasis.
- **Vocab anchors:** debate, culture, professional/academic, idioms, collocations.
- **Themes:** city vs country life — argued **(slice)**, a delicate negotiation
  **(slice)**, a cultural difference, work ethics, a nuanced complaint.

### C2 — `lines: 14–20`, full native range, irony/figurative, fine register control
- **Can-do:** handle humour, irony, specialised & abstract debate at native level.
- **Grammar focus:** full range; stylistic devices, fine register/tone control,
  figurative language, subtle implicature.
- **Vocab anchors:** idiom, irony markers, abstract/specialised lexis, register pairs.
- **Themes:** irony & humour in conversation **(slice)**, AI & creativity — abstract
  debate **(slice)**, a literary discussion, ambiguity & double meaning.

## French (target_lang: fr, base_lang: es)

### A1 — `lines: 6–10`, présent, 4–8 mots/ligne, registre très lent
- **Can-do:** se présenter, demander/donner des infos personnelles, nommer le quotidien.
- **Grammar focus:** `être`/`avoir` présent, articles définis/indéfinis, genre &
  nombre, pronoms sujets, négation `ne...pas`, questions `est-ce que`, `il y a`.
- **Vocab anchors:** salutations, nom, famille, nombres 1–20, jours, café/nourriture.
- **Themes:** rencontres/présentations **(slice)**, café & commander **(slice)**,
  la famille, nombres & heure, en classe, les achats.

### A2 — `lines: 8–12`, passé composé & futur proche, connecteurs et/mais/parce que
- **Can-do:** décrire la routine & le passé, faire des projets, échanges courts.
- **Grammar focus:** passé composé (avoir/être), futur proche, `pouvoir/devoir`,
  comparatif, adverbes de fréquence, prépositions de lieu, partitifs (du/de la).
- **Vocab anchors:** routine, week-end, directions, métiers, météo, santé.
- **Themes:** la routine **(slice)**, demander son chemin **(slice)**,
  le week-end dernier, faire des projets, chez le médecin, décrire quelqu'un.

### B1 — `lines: 10–14`, une subordonnée, registre naturel posé
- **Can-do:** raconter des expériences, donner son avis, gérer le voyage.
- **Grammar focus:** imparfait vs passé composé, futur simple, conditionnel présent,
  pronoms relatifs (qui/que/où), pronoms `en`/`y`, `il faut que` (intro).
- **Vocab anchors:** voyage, opinions, sentiments, problèmes, vie professionnelle.
- **Themes:** un voyage **(slice)**, avis sur un film/série **(slice)**,
  un souci à l'hôtel, projets d'avenir, décrire sa ville.

### B2 — `lines: 12–16`, subordination complexe, conditionnel, rythme quasi normal
- **Can-do:** débattre d'idées abstraites, peser le pour/contre, nuancer.
- **Grammar focus:** subjonctif présent, conditionnel passé, voix passive,
  discours indirect, connecteurs (cependant, bien que, malgré), `dont`.
- **Vocab anchors:** technologie, société, environnement, carrière, médias, abstrait.
- **Themes:** la technologie au quotidien — pour/contre **(slice)**, un désaccord
  au travail **(slice)**, l'actualité, l'environnement, un choix de carrière.

### C1 — `lines: 14–18`, complexe, sensible au registre, idiomatique
- **Can-do:** argumenter, changer de registre, employer l'idiome avec précision.
- **Grammar focus:** mise en relief (c'est...que), inversion, modalité nuancée,
  subjonctif passé, connecteurs avancés, locutions idiomatiques.
- **Vocab anchors:** débat, culture, professionnel/académique, idiomes, collocations.
- **Themes:** ville vs campagne — argumenté **(slice)**, une négociation délicate
  **(slice)**, une différence culturelle, l'éthique au travail, une plainte nuancée.

### C2 — `lines: 14–20`, gamme native complète, ironie/figuré, contrôle fin du registre
- **Can-do:** manier l'humour, l'ironie, le débat abstrait/spécialisé au niveau natif.
- **Grammar focus:** gamme complète; procédés stylistiques, contrôle ton/registre,
  langage figuré, implicite subtil, concordance des temps.
- **Vocab anchors:** idiome, marqueurs d'ironie, lexique abstrait/spécialisé, registres.
- **Themes:** ironie & humour **(slice)**, IA & créativité — débat abstrait **(slice)**,
  une discussion littéraire, l'ambiguïté & le double sens.

## Authoring rules (apply to every generated lesson)

1. **Original only.** Invent characters, situations, and lines. Do not reproduce
   or paraphrase any existing course's dialogue, translation, or notes.
2. **CEFR ceiling.** Stay within the level's grammar/vocab band; at most one
   "stretch" item (one notch up), embedded so its meaning is recoverable.
3. **Spiral.** Reuse anchor vocab/patterns from earlier levels naturally; record
   them in the `recycles:` frontmatter field so the SRS can target them.
4. **Modern situations.** Smartphones, streaming, messaging, apps, voicemail —
   never cassettes/fax/VCR/video stores/phone books.
5. **Speakable.** Short, natural turns suitable for TTS; punctuation engineers pace.
6. **Anticipation-ready.** Mark predictable lines as anticipation prompts (gap
   candidates) in `## Agent cues`.
